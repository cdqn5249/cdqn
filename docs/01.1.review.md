### 3.2 Morphic Inversion Claim (§7.2)

**Statement:**
> "kernel must perform a **Morphic Inversion**, zooming through the W1 symmetry down into the World 0 atomic energy authorizing that goal"

**New terminology:** "Morphic Inversion" and reference to "$\sigma^{-1}$ operator"

**Consistency check with 01.6:**

01.6 defines σ operator (substitution/inflation):
$$\sigma(A) = B \quad ; \quad \sigma(B) = BA$$

**Question:** Is σ invertible?

**Mathematical analysis:**
- σ maps single symbols to words (A→B, B→BA)
- σ is injective (different inputs give different outputs) ✓
- σ is NOT surjective onto Σ* (not all words are Fibonacci words) 
- σ⁻¹ exists on its image (the set of valid Sturmian words) ✓

**Inversion feasibility:**
- Given a valid word H in image(σ), can we recover the sequence of operations?
- Yes, if we know the decomposition into substitution steps
- Problem: Decomposition may not be unique without additional constraints

**Status:** ⚠️ **Mathematically plausible but non-trivial.** The existence of σ⁻¹ on valid words is reasonable, but efficient computation needs proof (appropriately deferred to †1).

## 4. PHYSICAL CORRECTNESS

### 4.1 Thermodynamic Claims (§3.1)

**Landauer principle citation:**
> "logical bit-logic transitions require non-zero energy release $(kT \ln 2)$"

**Verification:**
- Landauer's original result: E ≥ kT ln(2) per bit **erasure** (not transition) ⚠️
- k = Boltzmann constant (1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K)
- At T = 300K: E_min ≈ 2.87 × 10⁻²¹ J per bit erasure

**Precision issue:** Document says "bit-logic transitions" rather than "bit erasures." These are different:
- Reversible computing: Transitions can theoretically be energy-neutral
- Irreversible computing: Erasure requires Landauer energy

**Assessment:** Slightly imprecise wording but acceptable for conceptual framework. The key point (non-zero energy cost) remains correct under realistic (non-reversible) computing assumptions.

**Status:** ✅ **Acceptable with minor precision caveat.**
