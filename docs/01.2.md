### Abstract

Building upon the **Sovereign Physical Ontology** established in Paper 01.1, this document provides the formal architectural specification for the **Quang Number (Qn)**. We define the Qn as a 64-bit computational primitive structured as a **Triad of Dependencies**: Magnitude ($m$), Narrative ($n$), and Logic ($l$). 

This v1.12 refinement hardens the **Numerical Range Axiom** [Empirical Horizon], utilizing a 16.16 logarithmic bit-split to achieve a dynamic range of $\approx 10^{\pm 19,728}$, supported by rigorous mathematical verification. We introduce **Scale-Aware Logic** [Empirical Horizon] via **Bit 53**, providing the hardware-level capability to distinguish between **Zoom** and **Expansion** manifolds. By grounding these axioms in capability-based security, numerical analysis, and formal semantics, this specification establishes the technical baseline for the multi-dimensional execution host of the Chained and Distributed Quang Network (CDQN).

### 1. The Container: 64-Bit Word Alignment and Parallelism

The Quang Number is designed to manifest physical accountability (Paper 01.1) within standard 64-bit word containers, maximizing throughput on existing register files while enabling multi-dimensional data density.

#### 1.1 Hardware-Native Throughput and Alignment
Alignment to the 64-bit boundary ensures the Qn utilizes existing load/store units and cache line architectures without the "Abstraction Penalty" of non-standard bit-widths. 
*   **Empirical Support:** **Hennessy & Patterson (2024)** and **ARM Ltd. (2023)** manuals confirm that 64-bit aligned data access is the prerequisite for peak memory bandwidth, atomic consistency, and maximizing SIMD utilization in modern ARMv9-A and x86_64 architectures.
*   **Data Density Benefit:** While a 64-bit Qn increases fetch size relative to 32-bit scalars, the **Instruction Count Reduction** results in a net energy gain. Increasing data density per fetch directly addresses the modern "Memory Wall" (**Hennessy & Patterson, 2024**).

#### 1.2 Tri-Dimensional Partitioning (The m:n:l Triad)
The 64-bit word is partitioned into three distinct functional segments, representing the **Lamination** [Empirical Horizon] of Mathematical, Relational, and Behavioral dimensions.

| Bit Segment | Segment Name | Dimension | Architectural Role |

### 2. Segment `m`: The Mathematical Dimension (Magnitude)

The 32-bit Magnitude segment bypasses the iterative latencies of legacy IEEE 754 arithmetic by utilizing an unsigned **Logarithmic-Scaled Manifold** [Empirical Horizon].

#### 2.1 Logarithmic Arithmetic (LNS) and Numerical Range
The Magnitude segment treats values as logarithms, transforming multiplication and division into single-cycle additions and subtractions.
*   **The 16.16 Bit-Split:** We assume a fixed-point logarithmic split where 16 bits represent the integer part (Range) and 16 bits represent the fractional part (Precision).
*   **Numerical Range Axiom:** This split provides a dynamic range of $2^{65535} \approx \mathbf{10^{19,728}}$. 
*   **Mathematical Verification:** As established in the numerical analysis frameworks of **Higham (2002)** and **Muller et al. (2018)**, utilizing a 16-bit exponent base-2 allows for a representation of values far exceeding the $\approx 10^{308}$ limit of standard IEEE 754 double-precision.
*   **Empirical Support:** **Arnold (2022)** and **Coleman et al. (2002)** demonstrate that LNS provides wider dynamic range and lower power consumption for multiplication-heavy workloads than floating-point systems.

#### 2.2 Symbolic Exactness and Unsigned Logic
The 32 bits of segment `m` are **Unsigned**. By delegating "Charge" (Spin) to the Logic segment, the Qn gains dynamic range. Magnitude maintains **Symbolic Ratios** [Empirical Horizon] via deferred execution.
*   **Empirical Support:** **Boehm (2009)** and **Knuth (1997)** provide the foundation for using lazy evaluation in exact arithmetic. The Qn maintains 100% precision for rationals during the computational phase, eliminating "Rounding Drift."

### 3. Segment `n`: The Relational Dimension (Narrative)

The `n` segment (Bits 32-47) is the **Chronological Vocabulary Registry** [Empirical Horizon]. It interfaces the node's physical silicon identity with its computational intent.

#### 3.1 Vocabulary Inception and Contextual Keys
During the maturation process, the Narrative segment captures the SoC’s jitter and thermal pulses (Birth Pulse) and maps them to universal symbols.
*   **Contextual Key Encoding:** Bits [32-34] identify the "Operational Mode." Mode 0x0-0x3 (Subjective) interprets Spin as **Debt**; Mode 0x4-0x7 (Objective) interprets Spin as **Charge** (Paper 01.5).
*   **Cooperative Prefetching:** Extending **Ainsworth & Jones (2017)** and **Solihin (2020)**, the Narrative segment identifies "Relational Clusters," allowing the prefetcher to load chained neighbors into cache.

#### 3.2 Isotopic Identity and Shadow-Matching Defense
The remaining Narrative bits enable **Quang Isotopes** [Empirical Horizon]—primitives sharing magnitude but possessing distinct lineages.
*   **Security Mapping:** Following the systematic side-channel defense frameworks established by **Bernstein (2005)** for cryptographic implementations, the CDQN applies analogous principles of address randomization to isotopic identity. Because coordinates are derived from silicon noise, no two devices store a universal truth at the same physical address, preventing "Shadow-Matching" attacks.

### 4. Segment `l`: The Behavioral Dimension (Logic)

The `l` segment (Bits 48-63) acts as the **Scale-Aware Hardware Capability Mask** [Empirical Horizon].

#### 4.1 Bit-Level Partitioning: IBS, Spin, and Scale
1.  **Bits [48-51] (IBS Signature):** Identifies the **Invariant Behavioral State** (Void, Unity, Dual, etc.).
2.  **Bit [52] (Operational Spin):** The **Typed Charge System** [Empirical Horizon]. Defines Charge (0 = Presence; 1 = Reflected Intent).
3.  **Bit [53] (Scale Toggle):** Defines the **Manifold Depth** (0 = Expansion; 1 = Zoom). 
    *   **Capability Grounding:** This hardware gating mechanism is grounded in the capability-based security principles of **Levy (1984)** and **Miller et al. (2020)**. Bit 53 acts as a hardware-enforced permission that restricts operations to specific manifold depths, preventing underflow/overflow errors at the gate level.
4.  **Bits [54-63] (Permissive Mask):** Defines allowed operators associated with this primitive.

#### 4.2 The Resonance Predicate and Side-Channel Mitigation
Interaction between Qns is gated by a synchronous bitwise compatibility check.
*   **Precedence Rule:** The IBS-Type (bits 48-51) strictly takes precedence over Operational Spin (bit 52).
*   **Side-Channel Defense:** Security-hardened implementations utilize **Constant-Time Execution** (**Kocher, 1996**; **Ge et al., 2019**) for spin and scale detection, ensuring the Resonance Predicate does not leak metadata through timing variance.

#### 5.2 Operator Shorthand and Strict Inheritance

*   **Arithmetic Default Rule [Empirical Horizon]:** Shorthand symbols written in an operation strictly invoke the **Arithmetic Isotope** (Narrative = Origin; Logic = IBS).
*   **Strict Inheritance Axiom [Empirical Horizon]:** Shorthand operators interact with a subject using monadic composition rules (**Wadler, 1995**). The result strictly inherits and appends to the subject's Narrative context ($n_A \oplus \text{Event}$), ensuring context is not erased.

#### 5.3 Prohibition of Implicit Metadata Shift
No semantic ambiguity is permitted. 
*   **Enforcement:** Grounded in the type-theoretic formalisms of **Pierce (2002)** and the foundations for programming languages in **Harper (2016)**, if a resulting Qn requires a state differing from monadic inheritance, shorthand is **strictly forbidden**. The `cdqnLang` compiler must throw a syntax fault for implicit metadata overrides.
