### Abstract

While the internal geometric and chronological structures of the Quang Number (Qn) primitive have been formalized, the system requires a defined operational execution model. In legacy multi-core architectures, shared memory buses force systems to rely on synchronous locking mechanisms, leading to computational bottlenecks. This paper proposes the **Asynchronous Immutability Axiom** to theoretically enable native lock-free execution. Furthermore, it addresses the inherent risks of autonomous generative systems—where emergent complexity often leads to uncontrollable drift from operational reality. To operationalize and bound this creativity, the paper posits the necessity of a **Computational Harness**, specifically detailing the **VES (Variation, Evaluation, Selection) Harness**. This decoupled framework isolates high-speed combinatorial generation from strict mathematical auditing and subjective human steering. The architecture aims to ground probabilistic drift in formal, deterministic rules without obstructing the independent hardware sequence of Qn births. Finally, it introduces **Semantic Gravity Fields**, proposing a geometric methodology for how primitives natively acquire and orient meaning.

### 1. Introduction: The Concurrency Bottleneck

Contemporary operating systems frequently struggle with multi-threading at scale. When multiple computational threads attempt to read and write to the same data structures simultaneously, the OS must enforce strict synchronous locking to prevent data corruption. 

#### 1.1 The Synchronous Bottleneck in Legacy Architectures
**[Scientific Consensus]** Amdahl’s Law and parallel computing research demonstrate that maximum systemic speedup is strictly limited by the sequential, synchronous portions of the workload. Lock-free data structures are established as requisite for unlocking scalable parallelism.
* **Source 1:** *Hennessy, J. L., & Patterson, D. A. (2024). "Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach."*
* **Source 2:** *Herlihy, M., & Shavit, N. (2008). "The Art of Multiprocessor Programming."* 

#### 1.2 The Asynchronous Immutability Axiom
**[Empirical Horizon]** To pursue optimal throughput on consumer-grade SoCs, the CDQN architecture posits the **Asynchronous Immutability Axiom**. The hypothesis states that once a Qn primitive transitions from transient memory to the Monotonic Ledger (01.4), it is mathematically locked. Because it can never be altered natively (only masked via appended Tombstones), any processor thread can theoretically read it asynchronously without requiring a synchronous mutex lock. The primitive is thus structurally designed to support wait-free, highly concurrent read access at the hardware level.

### 3. Evaluation: Formal Verification and the Structural Firewall

The Evaluation phase acts as the active harness. It provides independent, deterministic auditing, assessing variations purely for mathematical consistency and contextual scope.

#### 3.1 Mitigating Algorithmic Drift and Hallucination
**[Scientific Consensus]** Standard generative models suffer from uncontrolled emergent drift and hallucinations. Interactive Theorem Provers (ITP) prevent logical drift by demanding that every computational step satisfies a strict formal proof. However, evaluating complex state-spaces in real-time risks exponential explosion, causing system timeouts.
* **Source 1:** *de Moura, L., et al. (2015). "The Lean Theorem Prover."*
* **Source 2:** *Biere, A., et al. (1999). "Symbolic Model Checking without BDDs."* Demonstrates the necessity of bounded evaluation to prevent state-space explosion during structural verification.

#### 3.2 Formal Verification via Bounded Qn Topology
**[Empirical Horizon]** The Evaluation module is theorized to function as an automated formal prover engine. Before a Variation is authorized for birth, the Evaluation engine logically traces its Markov Dependency Chain, verifying adherence to the Empirical Falsifiability Rule (01.3) and physical parameters (e.g., $z42$ limits). 
To prevent resource exhaustion from maliciously complex or non-terminating VSA bindings, the architecture proposes a **Computational Step-Bound** (analogous to execution "gas"). Proofs that exceed this hardcoded deterministic boundary are aborted, ensuring the Evaluation engine maintains $O(1)$ or bounded $O(\log N)$ throughput during hot-path generation.
* *Reference:* *Wood, G. (2014). "Ethereum: A Secure Decentralised Generalised Transaction Ledger."* (For theoretical constraints on non-terminating computational state limits).

#### 3.3 Epoch-Based Consolidation and Write-Contention Mitigation
**[Empirical Horizon]** It is a strict architectural mandate that **the VES Harness does not control the hardware clock.** The Evaluation engine acts strictly as a gating mechanism. When the $T_{epoch}$ metronome inherently strikes, the system consolidates whatever verified topology is queued. 

**[Open Implementation Challenge]:** If multiple asynchronous Evaluation threads race to queue verified Qns at the strict $T_{epoch}$ tick boundary, severe write-contention may occur. To guarantee safe, lock-free ledger batching at the hardware tick, the integration of advanced concurrency paradigms—such as Epoch-Based Reclamation (EBR) or Read-Copy-Update (RCU) protocols—is explicitly deferred to Series 03 OS driver specifications.
* *Reference:* *McKenney, P. E. (2004). "Exploiting Deferred Destruction: An Analysis of Read-Copy-Update Techniques in Operating System Kernels."*

The Selection phase constitutes the Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) integration, dictating how meaning is subjectively organized over time without mutating the underlying mathematical reality.

User selection in the CDQN framework merely alters the gradient overlay directing the Variation engine's *future* pathfinding. The underlying mathematical ledger remains objectively pure and uncorrupted by subjective human opinion.

High-Dimensional Computing confirms meaning can be natively represented by geometric vector distances. However, in high-dimensional spaces, distance metrics can converge, rendering proximity-based discrimination mathematically noisy (the "Curse of Dimensionality").

**[Empirical Horizon]** The CDQN architecture hypothesizes the **Semantic Gravity Field** to bridge rigid logic with linguistic nuance.

* When a new fractional Qn is birthed dynamically, its Markov Dependency Hash places it at a specific geometric distance from an Anchor Qn; it falls into that Anchor's "Semantic Gravity," natively acquiring meaning from proximity.
