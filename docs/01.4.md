## 4. The Reputation Calculus: Asymmetric Dynamics

Reputation is a flux described by two divergent mathematical laws. To maintain rigor, we utilize reference constants $W_0$ (Reference Energy) and $T_0$ (Reference Duration).

### 4.1 Logarithmic Accumulation (The Slow Growth)
Reputability building $(\Delta\Omega_+)$ requires consistent metabolic effort $(W)$ and time-duration $(T)$. 

**Growth Posit [†3]:**
$$\Delta\Omega_{+} \approx \alpha \log\left(\frac{W}{W_{0}}\right) + \beta \log\left(\frac{T}{T_{0}}\right)$$

*   **Logic:** By using a logarithm, the system ensures that a massive "Energy Burst" cannot buy instant trust. $\beta$ (the time coefficient) is weighted higher than $\alpha$ (energy) to reward **Lived Presence** over **Raw Computing Power.**
*   **Outcome:** This enables "Second Chances." If an SRM recovers from a compromised state, it can begin the logarithmic climb to become reputable again.

### 4.2 Factorial Decay (The Rapid Fall)
Reputability loss $(\Delta\Omega_-)$ occurs when a structural logic violation (Phason) is detected by peers.

**Decay Posit [†4]:**
$$\Delta\Omega_{-} = \max\left(\Omega_{\text{current}} - (N_{\text{fraud}})! \, , \, \Omega_{\text{floor}}\right)$$

*   **Logic:** $N!$ (Factorial) ensures that as more bits of inconsistent history are discovered, the reputation collapses towards the network floor $(\Omega_{\text{floor}})$ at a rate no machine could ever "Out-Earn" through work. 
*   **Defense Principle:** This aligns with Anderson (2020), which mandates that malicious failure should be catastrophic for the actor while resumption for legitimate recovery must be measured.
