### 3. DECIDABILITY BOUNDARY REFINEMENT

#### ✅ **CRITICAL CLARIFICATION: Section 5.2**

**Enhanced Content (v1.2):**

> "**Undecidable:** Determining if a DCTR is 'good' or 'malicious.'  
> **Decidable:** Determining if a DCTR creates a **Point-Set Overlap violation $(\delta \mathbf{x} \in \text{Kernel}(L))$**."

**Comparison to v1.1:**

| **Version** | **Decidable Property Definition** | **Precision** |
|-------------|-----------------------------------|---------------|
| **v1.1** | "Does this experience create a 'hole'?" | ★★★☆☆ (Informal) |
| **v1.2** | "$\delta \mathbf{x} \in \text{Kernel}(L)$" | ★★★★☆ (Semi-formal) |

**Mathematical Analysis:**

The notation "$\delta \mathbf{x} \in \text{Kernel}(L)$" suggests:
- $\delta \mathbf{x}$: Coordinate shift from DCTR application
- $\text{Kernel}(L)$: Null space of lattice operator $L$

**Interpretation:** A phason (lattice defect) occurs when the coordinate shift falls into the kernel (creates a "hole" in the geometric structure).

**Decidability Verification:**

| **Property** | **Formal Statement** | **Decidability** | **Complexity** |
|--------------|---------------------|------------------|----------------|
| Kernel membership | $\delta \mathbf{x} \in \text{Kernel}(L)$ | ✅ Decidable | $O(n^3)$ (Gaussian elimination) |
| Lattice connectivity | Graph reachability after DCTR | ✅ Decidable | $O(E + V)$ (BFS/DFS) |
| Semantic intent | "Is DCTR malicious?" | ❌ Undecidable | Rice (1953) |

**Verdict:** The refined definition is **mathematically sound** and **Rice-compliant**.
