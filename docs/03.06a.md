## VII. Glossary of Terms for Non-Technical Readers

We define the foundational concepts used in this specification to ensure interdisciplinary readability [1].

### A. Substructural and Attestation Terminology
*   **Fuzzy Commitment:** A cryptographic technique that binds a secret message (like a password) to noisy, fluctuating data (like physical silicon variations) using error-correcting codes, allowing the secret to be recovered securely without storing the original templates.
*   **Direct Anonymous Attestation (DAA):** A cryptographic protocol that allows a hardware chip to prove to a remote verifier that it is genuine and certified without revealing its unique identity or serial number.
*   **Saga Pattern (Compensating Transaction):** A distributed transactional pattern that partitions a long-running transaction into a series of local steps, executing a sequence of "destructor" actions to reverse the completed steps if a failure occurs.

### B. Compiler and Topos Terminology
*   **Subobject Classifier ($\Omega$):** The mathematical object in a sheaf topos that generalizes the classical concept of truth values, allowing for intuitionistic, multi-valued logic.
*   **Heyting Algebra:** A formal algebraic structure that governs intuitionistic logic, where the law of the excluded middle ($A$ or not $A$) does not necessarily hold, requiring all statements to be constructively proven.
*   **Strong Normalization:** A property of programming languages where every valid program is mathematically guaranteed to terminate (halt) regardless of the evaluation order, preventing infinite loop exploits.
