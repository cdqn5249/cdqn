## II. Axioms of the Base Space and Algebraic Exactness

To resolve the categorical failure of classical topological abelian groups ($\mathbf{TopAb}$ is not an abelian category) and the non-associativity of IEEE 754 floating-point registers, the $Qn$ primitive base space is defined over the category of **Condensed Sets**.

### A. The Profinite Site
Let $\mathbf{Prof}$ be the category of profinite sets, which are compact, totally disconnected Hausdorff topological spaces. Every $S \in \mathbf{Prof}$ is isomorphic to the inverse limit of an inverse system of finite discrete sets. We equip $\mathbf{Prof}$ with a Grothendieck topology where covering families are finite, jointly surjective maps:

$$\{ f_i: S_i \to S \}_{i \in I} \quad \text{such that} \quad \bigcup_{i \in I} f_i(S_i) = S$$

### B. The Condensed Sheaf Functor
A Quang Number $Q$ is defined as a condensed sheaf functor:

$$Q: \mathbf{Prof}^{\text{op}} \times \mathcal{C}^{\text{op}} \to \mathbf{Type}$$

satisfying the sheaf condition for the covering families on $\mathbf{Prof}$ and the open inclusion maps of our context category $\mathcal{C}$ [3]. This requires that for any finite, jointly surjective covering $\{S_i \to S\}$, the diagram:

$$Q(S) \to \prod_{i} Q(S_i) \rightrightarrows \prod_{i, j} Q(S_i \times_S S_j)$$

is an equalizer diagram in $\mathbf{Type}$ [4].

### C. The Abelian Category $\mathbf{CondAb}$
The category of condensed abelian groups $\mathbf{CondAb}$ forms an abelian category [5, 6]. For any morphism $f: A \to B$ in $\mathbf{CondAb}$:
*   The kernel exists and is computed pointwise: $(\text{Ker}(f))(S) = \text{Ker}(f(S))$.
*   The cokernel exists and is computed as the sheafification of the pointwise quotient.
*   Any bimorphism (a morphism that is simultaneously a monomorphism and an epimorphism) is a global isomorphism, resolving the $\mathbf{TopAb}$ failure [5].

## III. The Multi-Scale Metric and Truncation Invariants

To execute infinite-dimensional condensed sets on physical finite registers, the base space is truncated and equipped with an exact, non-leaking ultrametric space.

### A. The Profinite Truncation Functor ($\tau_z$)
Let $S = \varprojlim S_i$ be an infinite profinite set. We define the **Zoom $z$** as an index of a locally finite directed poset $I = \mathbb{N}$ representing our scale space. The **Profinite Truncation Functor** $\tau_z$ maps the infinite limit to its finite discrete representation $S_z$ at depth $z$:

$$\tau_z(S) = S_z$$

via the continuous, surjective projection mapping:

$$\pi_z: S \to S_z$$

The local evaluation of a $Qn$ stalk at zoom level $z$ is:

$$Q_z = Q(S_z)$$

### B. The $p$-adic Ultrametric Space
Every truncated quotient space $Q(S_z)$ is equipped with the $p$-adic absolute value $\|\cdot\|_p$ for a prime number $p$, satisfying the valuation:

$$\|x\|_p = p^{-v_p(x)}$$

This metric satisfies the **strong triangle inequality** (the ultrametric property) [7]:

$$\|x + y\|_p \le \max\left(\|x\|_p, \; \|y\|_p\right)$$

For any truncated approximations carrying error residuals $e_1, e_2 \le p^{-z}$, their arithmetic combination ($\oplus$) is strictly bounded:

$$\|e_1 \oplus e_2\|_p \le \max\left(\|e_1\|_p, \; \|e_2\|_p\right) \le p^{-z}$$

This prevents truncation errors from accumulating, guaranteeing zero numerical drift on registers [7].

## IV. Bidirectional Scaling via Ind-Pro Completions

To enable bidirectional scaling, the $Qn$ primitive is formulated over the **Ind-Pro-completion** category $\mathbf{Ind}\text{-}\mathbf{Pro}(\mathcal{C})$, which contains both arbitrary filtered limits and colimits [10].

### A. The Cohesive Adjoint Pair
The transition between the microscopic Pro-limits ($z \to -\infty$, totally disconnected) and the macroscopic Ind-limits ($z \to +\infty$, continuous fields) is managed by a cohesive adjoint functor pair $(\mathbb{L} \dashv \mathbb{R})$ [10]:

$$\mathbb{L}: \mathbf{Pro}(\mathcal{C}) \rightleftarrows \mathbf{Ind}(\mathcal{C}) : \mathbb{R}$$

This adjunction guarantees that when the system scales a state, the algebraic invariants and type-checking proofs are preserved continuously.

### B. The Spectral Sequence of Scales ($E_r$)
Let $C^\bullet$ be a filtered cochain complex of sheaves on the site $\mathbf{Prof}$, where the filtration $F_z C^\bullet$ is indexed by the Zoom scale $z \in I$. This filtration induces a spectral sequence of abelian groups $E_r^{p,q}$ equipped with differential operators [3, 11]:

$$d_r: E_r^{p,q} \to E_r^{p+r, \; q-r+1}$$

satisfying $d_r \circ d_r = 0$. The filtration degree corresponds to the Zoom scale $z$, while the page $r$ represents the successive stages of homological boundary resolution, allowing scale-dependent constraint filtering [3, 11].

### C. The Linear Resource Functor ($\Phi$)
During the bootstrap phase, the kernel evaluates the local **Resource Functor** $\Phi$ to profile the physical capabilities of the local hardware, treating resources as consumable, non-duplicable assets under **Linear Logic** [12, 13]:

The functor $\Phi$ uses this resource-bounded model to statically calculate the maximum stable spectral sequence page $r_{\max} = z_{\max}$ that the device can compute in linear time, preventing memory overflows and register exhaustion [13].

## V. Remainder Carriage and the Dual Halting Mechanism

To compute with exact remainders without incurring unbounded memory overhead, we define the algebraic remainder $r_z$ as the exact projective residual.

### A. The Hybrid Representation
The remainder $r_z = x - x_z \in \mathbf{Pro}(\mathcal{C})$ is represented as a hybrid structure:
*   *Application Level ($QnLang$):* An exact, symbolic coprime rational fraction in the $p$-adic field:
    $$r_z = \frac{a}{b} \in \mathbb{Q}_p$$
*   *Compiler Level ($QnIR$):* A lazy-evaluated $p$-adic power series:
    $$r_z = \sum_{i=z+1}^{\infty} a_i p^i$$

### B. Fast GCD Simplification Filters
Immediately following any algebraic operation on the remainders, the runtime executes a greatest common divisor (GCD) reduction using the **Schönhage Fast GCD algorithm** [15] and modern computer algebra complexity bounds [16]. This reduction is computed in near-linear time:

$$O(M(z \log p) \log z)$$

time, where $M(n)$ is the complexity of multiplying two $n$-bit integers [15, 16]. This restricts the bit-width of the remainder to a stable, linear growth rate $O(z)$ rather than an exponential rate $O(2^z) $ [15].

### C. The Halting Dualism
The execution of any arithmetic loop is governed by two symmetrical, highly optimized halting operators:
*   **The Hard Halt (Forced Truncation / Power Gating):** Triggered when the execution reaches the maximum allowable zoom level $z = z_{\max}$ defined by the hardware capacity, or when the idle window is long ($t_{\text{idle}} \ge \mathcal{T}_{\text{thermal}}$). The kernel halts the computation, power-gates the inactive core, and writes the uncomputed residual to disk as the exact remainder $r_{z_{\max}} \in Q_{\text{disk}}$, eliminating both dynamic switching and static leakage power [29, 30].
*   **The Soft Halt (Natural Termination):** Triggered when the projective residual $r_z = \frac{a}{b}$ collapses to exactly zero. The cdqnK kernel solves the Zero-Equivalence Problem in $O(1)$ constant time by reducing the fraction to its canonical normal form and testing if the numerator $a$ is exactly zero:
    $$a = 0 \implies r_z = 0$$
    The kernel immediately detects the zero-numerator state, bypasses all subsequent loop operations via branch-free stack selection, and terminates the register loop naturally, saving physical clock cycles and maximizing energy efficiency [31, 32].

## VI. Stochastic Transitions and the Concentric Semantic Rings

To model asynchronous, context-dependent, and probabilistic data flows across decentralized networks, state transitions are formulated over Markov Categories.

### A. Markov Categories ($\mathcal{M}$)
A **Markov Category** $\mathcal{M}$ is a symmetric monoidal category $(\mathcal{M}, \otimes, I)$ where every object $X \in \mathcal{M}$ is equipped with a compatible comonoid structure (copy morphism $\Delta_X: X \to X \otimes X$ and discard morphism $!_X: X \to I$). A morphism $f: X \to Y$ represents a **stochastic transition kernel** (or channel) rather than a deterministic function.

### B. Sheaf-Theoretic Markov Chains
A local stochastic transition is defined as a local section of a probability sheaf over $U$. If the local transition kernels agree on the overlaps, the **cohomological obstruction vanishes** ($H^1(X, \mathcal{F}) = 0$) [3]. 

The kernel utilizes a **cellular sheaf formulation** where the computation of the $H^1$ obstruction is mapped to a highly optimized, sparse-matrix linear programming step [17, 18]:

$$\text{Complexity} = O\big(\text{dim}(C^1) \cdot \text{dim}(C^0)\big)$$

allowing the system to detect and isolate local consensus splits and synchronization bottlenecks in linear or quadratic time.

### C. The Concentric Semantic Rings of Authority
To prevent unconstrained numerological associations, we enforce **Functorial Semantics** using a strict filtration of sub-sheaves of our semantic sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ [3]:

$$\mathcal{F}_0 \subseteq \mathcal{F}_1 \subseteq \mathcal{F}_2$$

*   **Ring 0 ($\mathcal{F}_0$): The Absolute Sheaf.** Contains the absolute, invariant constants of physical and logical reality (such as the Planck constant $h$). It has Absolute Authority, and its restriction maps are globally invariant.
*   **Ring 1 ($\mathcal{F}_1$): The Consensual Sheaf.** Contains the meanings given to numbers by shared human language, verified by the **Lambek Pregroup Grammar** [25] and **DisCoCat** models [26] in $QnLang$. Verbs are compiled as transition morphisms, and nouns are compiled as context objects.
*   **Ring 2 ($\mathcal{F}_2$): The Speculative Sheaf.** Contains speculative or unverified local models (zero global authority). A program attempting to inject a Ring 2 speculative state into a Ring 0 physical register is rejected by the compiler as a *morphism composition violation*.

### D. Substructural Memory: Linear-Typed Thunks & Affine Destructors
To prevent unforced remainders from generating space leaks and pointer-chase overhead:
*   **The Linear Type Constraint:** The remainder $r_z$ is restricted to a single-owner linear type ($r_z : Q(S_z) \multimap Q(S_{z+1})$) [12, 14]. This allows the kernel to execute **in-place memory updates** on the remainder registers. The old thunk pointer is immediately consumed and deallocated in $O(1)$ constant time [14].
*   **Thread-Local Stalk Isolation:** To prevent MESI cache-coherence storms on multi-core SoCs, the compiler restricts linear variables to single-core execution lanes, pinning the thread to a specific physical core.
*   **Affine Exception Recovery:** To prevent resource leaks during thread panics, the memory manager's logic is upgraded to **affine** (allowing resources to be consumed at most once). If a thread panics, the kernel forces the execution of the **affine destructors** to safely zero out and reclaim the memory block.

## X. Glossary of Terms for Non-Technical Readers

*   **Abelian Category:** A structured mathematical environment where calculations can be performed on the relationships (morphisms) between objects, similar to standard addition and subtraction of numbers. It guarantees that if a relationship is one-to-one and onto, it can be perfectly reversed.
*   **Profinite Set:** A topological space that is compact, completely disconnected, and can be broken down into a limit of finite, discrete pieces. An example is the Cantor set.
*   **Condensed Set ($\mathbf{CondSet}$):** A mathematical set that is defined by how it is viewed through the lens of profinite sets. It replaces standard topological spaces, allowing continuous and discrete algebra to coexist.
*   **Zoom Functor ($z$):** The computational operator that determines the depth of the profinite truncation. It acts as a "slider" that increases or decreases the mathematical resolution and precision of the data.
*   **Ultrametric Space:** A metric space where the distance between points satisfies a "strong" version of the triangle inequality. In this space, distances do not accumulate during addition, and triangles are always either isosceles or equilateral.
*   **Spectral Sequence:** A mathematical tool that performs calculations on a space by taking successive, layered approximations (called pages) corresponding to different scales or resolutions of the space.
*   **Markov Category:** A mathematical environment where calculations are performed on "probabilistic paths" or "channels" instead of standard, deterministic functions. It allows us to calculate probability and entropy using simple diagrams.
*   **Direct Anonymous Attestation (DAA):** A cryptographic protocol that allows a hardware chip to prove to a remote verifier that it is genuine and certified without revealing its unique identity or serial number.
*   **Sovereign Attribution:** A security paradigm where every software action is cryptographically and physically bound to a specific physical computer (the tool) and a legally responsible human being (the operator).
*   **Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling (DVFS / P-states):** A hardware-level technique that dynamically down-scales the operating voltage and frequency of a processor core to match its real-time workload, flattening temperature oscillations ($\Delta T$) to prevent physical Thermal Cycling (TC) fatigue.
*   **Power Gating (C-states):** A power-saving technique that completely cuts off the supply voltage to an inactive processor core, eliminating both dynamic switching and static leakage current during long idle periods.
