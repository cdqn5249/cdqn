Quang Number (Qn) Foundational Axioms

Research Question

Can numbers abstract anything?

First Principle (FP0)

Every first-class entity manipulated inside the CDQN formalism SHALL be represented as one or more Quang Numbers (Qns).

Foundational Axioms

IDNameNormative statement
QA0Finite Constructive RealizabilityEvery Qn MUST have a finite representation, finite constructive definition and finite validation procedure.
QA1IdentityEvery Qn MUST possess a unique immutable identity.
QA2Explicit Constructive DefinitionEvery Qn SHALL be completely described by an explicit constructive specification.
QA3DependenciesEvery non‑primitive Qn MUST declare the finite set of Qns required for its construction.
QA4ConstraintsEvery Qn MUST declare the constraints defining its validity domain.
QA5Constructive Information PreservationEvery constructive transformation SHALL preserve, transform or explicitly declare the elimination of every essential constructive information.
QA6ImmutabilityA constructed Qn MUST NOT be modified. Any modification creates a new Qn.

Derived Concepts

  • Q(exp): complete constructive representation.
  • Zoom (Z): presentation scale without altering the underlying Q(exp).
  • Precision (P): executable numerical projection.
  • Remainder Carrying: derived from QA5 to avoid silent information loss.
  • Qm + Qs → QnIR.
  • Execution Collapse: deterministic projection from QnIR to machine registers.
  • Qdisk: validated persistent constructive knowledge.
  • Qreuse: validated reuse from Qdisk using metrics.
  • QnLang is intentionally not Turing-complete as a consequence of QA0.

Scope

This specification defines only the ontology of Quang Numbers. Q0–Q9, Qm, Qs, Q(exp), QnIR, Qdisk, Qreuse and QnLang are specified in separate documents.
