### Abstract
This paper establishes the fifteenth step in the CDQN theoretical framework by proposing the mathematical, systems, and cryptographic specification of **Sovereignty and Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI)** for the **Stage 4 High-Level Language ($QnLang_4$)** [2, 17]. Building upon the physical attestation layers developed in Chapter 2.13 and the parallel execution validation in Chapter 3.04a, we address the challenge of anchoring logical computations and cryptographic proofs directly to a legally recognized human operator without the use of biometric databases or centralized identity authorities [4, 5, 6]. 

We propose the syntactic rules and compile-time verification passes to bind our abstract physical witness operations to the **Human Accountability Anchor ($\mathcal{A}_{\text{human}}$)** [17]. We specify the **Symmetric Monoidal Semantic Hash ($\mathcal{H}_{\text{smc}}$)** of a parsed short story, demonstrating how the compiler maps qualitative human-memorable concepts to a stable, non-biometric, and hardware-bound Fuzzy Commitment [5, 27, 28]. To prevent credential-stealing exploits, we construct the **Speculative Multi-Agent Entropy Union** and the **High-Dimensional Lattice Network**, allowing nodes to cooperatively generate threshold signatures and expand their cryptographic lattices to achieve exponential security amplification [8, 9, 10, 11]. Furthermore, we formalize the **Sovereign Code Economy**, specifying how the `import package` economic gateways negotiate licensing covenants (USSL v1.0) and settle transaction fees natively via the exact $Qn$ ledger primitives [12, 13]. This framework serves as the human-attribution and licensing mechanism of the cdqn ecosystem.

## I. Introduction and Continuity from Stage 3 Specifications

To construct a high-level programming language capable of expressing exact, multi-scale algebraic computations, we propose to verify each compiler ascension stage iteratively using physical use-case validation gates [1, 2].

### A. The Physical Attestation and Cloned-Substrate Vulnerabilities
In the Stage 3 transactional co-design (`CDQN-03.04a` v1.0.2), the compiler successfully verified the **Saga-Based Speculative Rollback Engine**, proving that we can execute and reverse speculative commits in $O(1)$ constant time with zero data-remanence or speculative timing side-channel leaks [03.04a.md, 34]. Furthermore, in Chapter 2.13, we specified the **`wasi-hardware` System Import and DAA/PUF Anchor**, proving that the virtual machine can verify the physical genuineness of the executing silicon [02.13.md, 34].

However, executing these physical attestation proofs introduces a final, systems-level security boundary:
*   While the physical attestation proves that the executing hardware is genuine and certified, a secure hardware tool is legally and ethically non-accountable on its own [17].
*   In human society, accountability and legal non-repudiation must always backtrack to a recognized legal person (the operator) [17].
*   If the system relies on biometric data (such as fingerprints or facial scans) to bind the human to the hardware, it introduces severe privacy, regulatory, and security liabilities [4].
*   If a biometric template is compromised, it cannot be revoked or re-keyed, resulting in permanent identity theft [4].
*   Conversely, if the system relies on a simple password or seed, an attacker who steals the passphrase can use their own certified CDQN hardware to hijack your identity [02.13.md].

### B. Proposing the Self-Sovereign Identity and Human Accountability Anchor
To resolve these identity-takeover and biometric-leak vulnerabilities, we propose to govern the Stage 4 syntax of $QnLang_4$ using the **Human Accountability Anchor ($\mathcal{A}_{\text{human}}$)** and **Password-Hardened Fuzzy Commitments** [5, 17]. 
*   **The Invariant Shield:** By combining a low-entropy, human-memorized mnemonic seed or semantic short story with the noisy, physical manufacturing variations of the silicon substrate ($PU\text{-}\mathcal{F}_c$), the compiler's type-checker can statically prove human-hardware attribution before execution [5, 17].
*   We hypothesize that this multi-factor, non-biometric model completely isolates the user's private identity while maintaining absolute, non-repudiable accountability, allowing us to enforce a "quiet" and legally binding execution environment [17, 21]. `[Empirical Horizon]`

## II. The Non-Biometric Sovereign Identity Model

To perform secure hardware-human binding without collecting or processing biometric templates, we formulate our high-level identity primitives under the algebraic laws of Fuzzy Commitments [5, 6].

### A. The Symmetric Monoidal Semantic Hash ($\mathcal{H}_{\text{smc}}$)
Grounded in Yevgeniy Dodis et al. (2004) and Bob Coecke et al. (2010), we propose to map the human operator's personalized, memorable story (e.g., *"My blue cat drinking green tea in Hanoi at midnight"*) to a high-entropy cryptographic seed [5, 28]:
1.  **The Pregroup Reduction:** The compiler's parser reads the sequential story tokens, maps them to the pregroup lexicon, and executes the monoidal contractions to verify grammatical correctness [27].
2.  **The SMC Graph Mapping:** The grammatical reduction constructs a unique, coordinate-free Symmetric Monoidal Category (SMC) hypergraph representing the exact semantic relationships between the $x$ subjects [2, 23].
3.  **The Structural Hash:** The compiler executes a **Symmetric Monoidal Hash ($\mathcal{H}_{\text{smc}}$)** over this graph [23]:
    $$\mathcal{H}_{\text{smc}}(f_S) = \text{Hash}\big(\text{Topology}(f_S)\big) \in \{0, 1\}^n$$
    Because synonyms map to the exact same canonical parent nodes in our Ontological Sheaf, minor variations in word selection (e.g., *"My azure kitten sipping warm tea"*) yield the identical semantic hash, providing a robust, human-friendly, and non-biometric "fuzzy" matching layer [2, 39].

### B. The Password-Hardened Fuzzy Commitment
Grounded in Behzad Abdolmaleki et al. (2025), this high-entropy semantic hash is bound directly to the physical hardware's manufacturing variations ($w$) using an $[n, k]$ error-correcting binary block code $C$ [6]:
1.  **The Commitment Construction:** During the onboarding phase, the capability manager generates the helper data (the sketch) $\delta$ [5, 6]:
    $$\delta = w \oplus \mathcal{H}_{\text{smc}}(f_S)$$
2.  **The Syndrome Verification:** At runtime, the user inputs their story $S'$, and the hardware sweeps the active, noisy PUF state $w'$ [5, 19]. The system computes the speculative syndrome to resolve the Graph Edit Distance (GED) [5, 19]. If the GED falls within the error-correcting threshold $t$, the decoder reconstructs the exact same anchor key [5, 19]:
    $$K_{\text{anchor}} = \text{Hash}\big(\mathcal{H}_{\text{smc}}(f_S), \; w\big)$$

Because the reconstruction of $K_{\text{anchor}}$ requires **both** the unique physical silicon structure of your specific computer $w$ (the tool) and the memorized story in your mind $P$ (the operator), **the key is physically unclonable and legally non-repudiable without collecting a single byte of biometric data [5, 19].**

## III. Speculative Multi-Agent Entropy Union and High-Dimensional Lattices

To prevent physical node cloning and credential-stealing exploits across heterogeneous distributed networks, the compiler implements a collective post-quantum threshold security model [8, 9].

### A. The Threshold DKG Consensus (The Multi-Agent Shield)
Grounded in Rosario Gennaro et al. (2007) and Dan Boneh et al. (2020), **Threshold Cryptography** and **Distributed Key Generation (DKG)** allow multiple independent, untrusted nodes to cooperatively generate signatures without exposing their individual private shares [8, 9]:
1.  **The Quorum Registration:** During account initialization, the compiler's parser enforces that the master identity key is registered as a **Threshold post-quantum signature ($t$-of-$n$)** [2, 9].
2.  **The Guardian Distribution:** The $n$ key shares are distributed across your pre-registered, independent guardian nodes (such as your smartphone, your tablet, or trusted peers on the network) [8].
3.  **The Recovery Gate:** To authorize a hardware migration (a Hot-Swap) on a new computer, you must type your story to generate $c$, **but you must also request cryptographic approval from at least $t$ of your pre-authorized guardian devices [8, 9].** Even if an attacker learns your story, they cannot hijack your identity because they do not physically possess your other guardian devices to reach the required $t$-of-$n$ consensus threshold [5].

### B. High-Dimensional Lattice Expansion and Security Amplification
Grounded in Oded Regev (2009) and Vadim Lyubashevsky et al. (2013), post-quantum security is established by representing physical witnesses as n-dimensional coordinate vectors $\vec{v}_i \in \mathbb{Z}^n$ in a discrete subgroup space (a lattice) [10, 11]:
*   **The Basis Matrix:** When $N$ nodes link together at time $t$, their coordinate vectors are combined to form a high-dimensional basis matrix $\mathbf{B}$ representing the joint lattice network [10, 11].
*   **Dimensional Expansion:** This lattice mapping guarantees that **security amplifies exponentially with the number of linked nodes ($N$) [02.12.md]:** as the quantity of connected nodes grows ($N \to \infty$), the basis matrix $\mathbf{B}$ expands, adding new dimensions to the lattice network. This makes the execution of a brute-force decryption or emulation attack exponentially more difficult for both classical and quantum adversaries, as the complexity of solving the Shortest Vector Problem (SVP) scales exponentially with $N$ [10, 11]. The network does not require heavy, unmanaged cryptographic overhead; **it secure-hardens itself natively simply through the topological addition of new physical nodes [41, 42].**

## IV. Speculative Piecewise Verification of High-Precision Proofs

To prevent resource-constrained, non-magnitude nodes (where the maximum zoom scale $z_{\max} < z_{\text{target}}$) from experiencing memory exhaustion during verification, the compiler implements a piecewise, multi-pass validation loop [14, 33].

### A. Splitting High-Precision DAA Signatures into $x$-Step Chunks
Grounded in Andreas Abel (2004) and Rodney G. Downey & Alexander Melnikov (2025), infinite profinite algebraic structures can be evaluated and decided deterministically via finite, sequential approximations at controlled depths, where structurally bounded induction guarantees termination [14, 15]:
1.  **The Divisibility Invariant:** Because the DAA group signature is compiled over the profinite site, a high-precision proof ($z_{\text{target}}$) is projectively divisible into a tree-like hierarchy of finite, nested quotients $S_i$ [33].
2.  **The $x$-Step Loop:** The lesser node does not construct the entire $z_{\text{target}}$ tree in memory [33]. Instead, it uses the **SMC SEMP Metric Matrix ($\mathcal{M}_{\text{semp}}$)** to split the proof into a sequence of $x$ local verification steps [40]:
    $$x = \lceil \frac{z_{\text{target}}}{z_{\max}} \rceil$$
    During each step $i$, the lesser node loads a localized, truncated chunk of the persistent remainder $Q_{\text{disk}}$ from the persistent disk, evaluates the bilinear pairing equation natively on the stack, and checks the local gluing condition ($H^1 = 0$) [14, 15, 41, 42].

### B. Non-Blocking Asynchronous Consensus via Speculative Saga Rollbacks
Grounded in Hector Garcia-Molina & Kenneth Salem (1987) and H. T. Kung & John T. Robinson (1981), optimistic concurrency control and compensating transactions (Sagas) allow parallel execution threads to run-ahead speculatively, eliminating network-wide synchronization stalls:
1.  **The Speculative Run-Ahead:** When the capable node proposes a high-precision transaction, the network speculatively commits the state $x_{t+1}$ to local transient buffers immediately, allowing the capable nodes to continue executing subsequent transactions at maximum hardware frequency [34, 35].
2.  **Asynchronous Verification:** The lesser node executes its $x$ sequential verification steps **completely asynchronously in the background** as a low-priority task [34, 35].
3.  **The Speculative Rollback:** If the lesser node eventually detects a cryptographic or cohomological inconsistency ($H^1 \neq 0$) at step $i$, it triggers the **Saga-Based Speculative Rollback [34, 37].** The active Capability Supervisor cancels the speculative pipeline, and the host-side affine destructors ($!$) scrub and zero out the registers across the network, restoring global topological consistency [32, 37].

## V. The Sovereign Code Economy and Functorial Licensing Covenants

To prevent un-auditable third-party dependencies and corporate API enclosure, $QnLang_4$ implements a decentralized, peer-to-peer **Sovereign Code Economy** [3, 12, 13].

### A. The `import package` Economic Gateway
We propose to represent every software package as a self-contained, cryptographically signed, and immutable collection of module graphs bound to a specific license (such as the Universal Sovereign Source License (USSL) v1.0) and a unique structural hash [3]:

```qn
-- Formally importing an economic package under the USSL v1.0 license
import package core_ledger@1.0.0 license "USSL-1.0" exposing { exact_transfer }
```

When a program compiles this import, the local **Cost Functor ($\mathcal{K}$)** executes a multi-objective optimization [5, 40]. If the functor calculates that compiling and executing the code locally on standard silicon (Option C) is cheaper and more secure than paying a remote node's computation and latency costs, the local node compiles and executes it natively [5, 40].

### B. Compile-Time Deontic Compliance via the Regulation Sheaf
Grounded in *A Categorical Framework for Compliance Architecture* (Bonchi et al., 2024) and G. R. Gangadharan et al. (2012), software licenses can be formalized and verified using deontic logic [12, 13]:
1.  **The Compliance Check:** During the compilation pass, the compiler's type-checker evaluates the **Regulation Sheaf ($\mathcal{R}_{\text{reg}}$)** over the imported package's dataflow graphs [12, 13].
2.  **The Deontic Constraints:** The type-checker uses deontic logic rules to statically verify that the compiled program complies with the copyleft and open-core requirements of the USSL v1.0 [12].
3.  **The Compiler Guard:** If a developer attempts to compile a program (morphism $f$) that violates the USSL constraints (such as attempting to enclose an open-core primitive within a closed-source block), **the type-checker blocks compilation at the syntactic boundary with a licensing type error [12].** The program is structurally barred from generating the target bytecode, protecting the author's work and lineage from industrial enclosure [12].

If the imported package requires a license fee, the local node settles the transaction fee natively on-chain using the exact $Qn$ ledger primitives [1.13]. This contract is enforced statically by the compiler's **Regulation Sheaf ($\mathcal{R}_{\text{reg}}$)**, completely replacing the corporate API economy with a decentralized, peer-to-peer, and mathematically verifiable Code Exchange [12].

## VIII. Glossary of Terms for Non-Technical Readers

We define the foundational concepts used in this specification to ensure interdisciplinary readability [1].

### A. Substructural and Attestation Terminology
*   **Fuzzy Commitment:** A cryptographic technique that binds a secret message (like a password) to noisy, fluctuating data (like physical silicon variations) using error-correcting codes, allowing the secret to be recovered securely without storing the original templates.
*   **Direct Anonymous Attestation (DAA):** A cryptographic protocol that allows a hardware chip to prove to a remote verifier that it is genuine and certified without revealing its unique identity or serial number.
*   **Threshold Signatures ($t$-of-$n$):** A cryptographic security model where a key is split into $n$ shares, requiring a minimum of $t$ shares to agree before a transaction can be approved, eliminating single points of failure.

### B. Compiler and Licensing Terminology
*   **Point-Free Programming:** A programming paradigm where computations are expressed entirely through the direct composition of functions without referencing intermediate variable names, reducing compiler parsing complexity.
*   **Regulation Sheaf ($\mathcal{R}_{\text{reg}}$):** The type-checking constraint in QnLang that mathematically monitors and enforces software license compliance (deontic logic) at compile-time, ensuring copyleft protection.
*   **Sovereign Code Economy:** A decentralized, peer-to-peer data-transit system where importing packages is modeled as a monoidal functor that negotiates licensing covenants and settles transaction fees natively via $Qn$ ledger primitives under cryptographic, hardware-anchored provenance.
