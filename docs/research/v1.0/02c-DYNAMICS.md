## 2. The Quantale Hamiltonian (H)
In the CDQN Formalism, interaction is not a sequence of instructions but a physical path. The state of the system is governed by a Hamiltonian function that sums the spatial, temporal, and social tensions of the active CDU stack.

### 2.1 The Energy Equation
For a selected set of CDUs (S), the total energy **H** is defined as:

$$H(S) = \mathcal{T}_{\text{spatial}}(S) + \mathcal{T}_{\text{temporal}}(S) + \lambda \mathcal{I}(S)$$

Where:
*   **Spatial Tension:** The Dirichlet Energy of the superposed Continuous Lattices within the CDUs, as derived in `02b`.
*   **Temporal Tension:** The divergence of the CDU's Ouroboros chain from the system's causal axis.
*   **Integrity Penalty (I):** The resistance generated by the Reputation Lattice of the CDU during a state-alignment check.

### 2.2 Minimization Principle
The system naturally evolves toward a state where the change in **H** is zero. This evolution is driven by the **User Attractor (Gamma)**, which acts as a potential well that pulls compatible CDUs into a stable lamination.

## 3. Phase Transition Mechanics
We formally define how the internal state of a CDU shifts between elasticity states based on the local energy density.

### 3.1 Fluid-to-Crystal Annealing
When an Entity identifies a high-resonance superposition between CDUs, it performs **Thermodynamic Annealing**:
1.  **Fluid Phase:** The CDU's internal Lattice is high-entropy and adaptive.
2.  **Cooling:** As Geometric Tension is minimized, the system reduces the local "Temperature" parameter of the CDU.
3.  **Crystallization:** The CDU is "Frozen" into an inelastic Crystal state. This consumes a fixed **Quantale Cost** but ensures that the resulting truth is invariant and requires zero energy to maintain in future laminations.

### 3.2 The Shattering Law (Plasma)
If a Crystal CDU is subjected to an external force (New Input) that creates a Geometric Tension exceeding its **Tensile Strength**, the CDU does not bend; it **Shatters**.
