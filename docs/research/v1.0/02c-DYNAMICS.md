## 2. The Quantale Hamiltonian (H)
The state of the CDQN system is governed by a Hamiltonian function that sums the spatial, temporal, and social tensions of the Laminated Sheaf.

### 2.1 The Energy Equation
For a selected set of Lattices **S**, the total energy **H** is defined as:

$$H(S) = \mathcal{T}_{spatial}(S) + \mathcal{T}_{temporal}(S) + \lambda \mathcal{I}(S)$$

Where:
*   **Spatial Tension:** The Dirichlet Energy derived in `02b`, measuring the "tear" between overlapping layers.
*   **Temporal Tension:** The divergence from the Ouroboros causal axis.
*   **Integrity Penalty (I):** The resistance of the system's immune response to low-reputation or unknown states.

### 2.2 Minimization Principle
The system naturally evolves toward a state where the change in **H** is zero. This evolution is driven by the **User Attractor (Gamma)**, which creates a potential well in the energy landscape.

## 3. Phase Transition Mechanics
We formally define how Digital Matter shifts between states of elasticity based on the local temperature (Energy Density).

### 3.1 Fluid-to-Crystal Annealing
When the system identifies a high-resonance superposition (Meaning), it performs **Thermodynamic Annealing**.
1.  **Fluid State:** High entropy, high plasticity. The NPU explores multiple lamination configurations.
2.  **Cooling:** As tension is minimized, the system reduces the local "Temperature" parameter.
3.  **Crystallization:** The data is "Frozen" into an inelastic Crystal Lattice. This consumes a fixed **Quantale Cost** but ensures future operations on this data are near-zero energy (Invariant).

### 3.2 The Shattering Law (Plasma)
If a Crystal Lattice is subjected to an external force (New Data) that exceeds its **Tensile Strength** (Reputation/Mass), the crystal does not "bend"; it **Shatters**.
*   **Result:** The state transitions to **Plasma** (Error/Rejection), triggering a system-wide re-evaluation of the Sheaf.
