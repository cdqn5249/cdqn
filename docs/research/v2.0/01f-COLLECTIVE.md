## 1. Abstract
The "Cloud Computing" era has introduced a significant **Sovereignty Bottleneck**: to achieve high-performance logic and reliable disaster recovery, individuals and institutions must rent "tenancy" on centralized servers. This model requires users to replicate their data across unowned substrates, leading to a state of **Data Diaspora**. This paper identifies the thermodynamic and security risks of data cloning and proposes **Substrate Pooling** as a viable alternative. By deriving the **Axiom of Holonic Resonance**, we demonstrate a system where independent nodes dynamically cluster into specialized families—Swarms, Service Nodes, and Anchors—to provide **Durable Execution**. We introduce the **Reconstruction Protocol** as a zero-trust verification mechanism, proving that collective performance and resilience can be achieved through topological sections rather than data clones.

## 3. The Diagnostic: The Disaster Recovery Paradox

Current shared computing environments force a compromise between **Security** (data isolation) and **Availability** (data redundancy).

### 3.1 The Failure of the "Full Backup"
To prevent data loss, the industry relies on **Asynchronous Replication**. 
*   **The Risk:** Every backup is a "Clone." In 2026, this creates an unmanageable attack surface where "Deleted" data persists indefinitely on forgotten cloud servers. 
*   **The Cost:** Maintaining millions of data clones "warm" consumes vast amounts of energy without increasing the underlying truth-mass of the information.

### 3.2 The Trust Gap in Shared Computing
When a user offloads a task to a shared cluster (Cloud or Grid), they must either "Trust" the provider or use computationally expensive mathematical proofs to verify the result. This creates an **Opacity Gap** that makes real-time, high-stakes sovereign simulation (e.g., in robotics or finance) impossible to audit at the edge.
