## 3. The Diagnostic: The Chaos of Unstructured Data

Current IT systems permit the creation of "Impossible Graphs"—data structures so complex and non-linear that they become unrecognizable if they are broken into pieces.

### 3.1 The Limits of the Kelly-Ulam Conjecture
The **Graph Reconstruction Conjecture** (1942) suggests that a graph can be rebuilt from its sub-graphs, but this remains unproven for "General Graphs."
*   **The Constraint:** Because modern software (C++, Python, Rust) allows for arbitrary data pointers and "Spaghetti" relationships, current digital records are **Non-Reconstructible.** 
*   **The Result:** If a server loses a "Fragment" of a database, the remaining pieces often cannot mathematically prove what the original whole looked like, leading to permanent data loss or corruption.

### 3.2 The High-Entropy Failure (Space & Security)
In extreme environments (Space, High-Radiation, or Targeted Cyber-Warfare), data fragments constantly. Traditional "Redundancy" (making three clones) fails because a pirate or a cosmic ray can corrupt all three clones in different ways. Without a **Rigid Geometry**, the system has no way to determine which version is the truth.
