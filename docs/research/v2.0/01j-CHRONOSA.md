## 3. The Diagnostic: The Alignment Gap

Current interfaces are disconnected from the physical and social consequences of the data they present to the user and the network.

### 3.1 The Risk of Protocol Damping
In the **Silent Forest** (`01i`), any node that emits high-entropy noise or false data is naturally damped (silenced) by the protocol.
*   **The Problem:** Traditional UIs do not warn the user when they are about to share "Dissonant" data that contradicts established facts.
*   **The Result:** A user might accidentally "burn" their local node's reputation in the **CDQN** by sharing a hallucination, leading to an immediate loss of network visibility and access to shared performance (`01f`).

### 3.2 The Failure of Statistical Compliance
Traditional LLMs are trained to maximize "User Satisfaction" rather than "Physical Integrity."
*   **The Problem:** An AI that ignores the node's **Causal Mass** will provide an agreeable lie if it fits the prompt's probability.
*   **The Consequence:** The user loses sovereignty because their guide (the AI) is leading them toward a state of thermodynamic isolation from the rest of the **CDQN**.
