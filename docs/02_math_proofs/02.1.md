Contemporary computer architectures typically compile programs down to register structures where high-level semantic rules, bounds, and contexts are discarded—a logical gap frequently characterized as the "Semantic Void" [1.2]. All formalisms, conjectures, and proposed proofs in this subseries represent preliminary mathematical models submitted for academic scrutiny, formal verification, and peer validation by the broader mathematics and theoretical computer science communities.

### 2.1 Proposed Definition 1: An Exploratory Physical Metric Space Model
Standard computer science models typically treat physical hardware as an infinite, error-free Turing machine tape [1.2]. To ensure our proposed proof models remain grounded in physical constraints and satisfy dimensional scaling requirements [1.99], we explore representing the physical substrate as a compact, normalized metric space.

We define a dimensionless, scaled coordinates space $(\mathbb{P}, d_{\mathbb{P}})$ where physical values are normalized by characteristic system constants: let $\tau_0$ be the system clock reference interval, let $V_T$ be the characteristic thermal voltage of the silicon junction ($V_T = k_{\text{B}} T / q_{\text{e}}$), and let $E_{\text{therm}}$ be the characteristic thermal noise energy floor ($E_{\text{therm}} = k_{\text{B}} T$) [1.39].

To resolve the continuous-discrete topological mismatch identified in initial audits, we model the physical substrate $\mathbb{P}$ as a **discrete, finite cell complex** $\mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}}$ (e.g., a CW complex or simplicial complex) representing the discretized lattice of memory locations, register gates, and clock stages [1.101, 1.102]. 

Let $\mathbb{P} \equiv \mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}}$ be the finite cell complex defined by [1.30, 1.39, 1.99, 1.102]:

$$
\mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}} = \mathcal{T}_{\text{norm}} \times \mathcal{V}^k_{\text{norm}} \times \mathcal{E}^m_{\text{norm}}
$$

Where:
*   $\mathcal{T}_{\text{norm}} = \{ 0, 1, \dots, N_{\text{max}} \}$ represents the discrete normalized clock cycles, scaled by $\tau_0$ [1.77, 1.99].
*   $\mathcal{V}^k_{\text{norm}} = [v_{\text{lower}}/V_T, v_{\text{upper}}/V_T]^k \cap \mathbb{Q}$ represents the normalized, rationalized potential states across $k$ discrete logic gates [1.23, 1.99].
*   $\mathcal{E}^m_{\text{norm}} = [0, e_{\text{max}}/E_{\text{therm}}]^m \cap \mathbb{Q}$ represents the normalized local thermodynamic entropy states [1.3, 1.99].
*   The metric $d_{\mathbb{P}}$ on the cells $\sigma \in \mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}}$ is the discrete metric induced by the face relation $\sigma \le \tau$ and normalized coordinate intervals [1.102]:

    $$
    d_{\mathbb{P}}(\sigma_1, \sigma_2) = \inf \left\{ \sum \text{dist}_{\text{cell}}(\sigma_i, \sigma_{i+1}) \right\}
    $$

Because the coordinates are scaled by physical constants, the metric is dimensionally consistent [1.99]. Additionally, physical transitions are bounded, suggesting that the physical state space may be modeled as **compact and bounded** [1.1, 1.39].

## Section 3: Defining the Computer Science State Set $\mathbb{C}$

We analyze how the computer science execution state ($\mathbb{C}$) can be defined to ensure that physical hardware transitions do not violate high-level semantic rules.

### 3.1 The Inadequacy of Cartesian Subsets
In typical relational modeling, one might define the computer science state as a simple Cartesian subset:

$$
\mathbb{C}_{\text{naive}} \subseteq \mathbb{P} \times \mathbb{M} \times \mathbb{L}_{\text{logic}}
$$

We hypothesize that this formulation is **insufficient**. A simple subset allows arbitrary, un-correlated combinations of states and fails to mathematically guarantee that a physical change in the silicon ($\mathbb{P}$) is structurally governed by a mathematical boundary ($\mathbb{M}$) or authorized by a logical capability ($\mathbb{L}_{\text{logic}}$). It leaves potential "gaps" where the physical hardware can execute transitions that violate the upper logical constraints, re-introducing the Semantic Void [1.2].

### 3.2 Proposed Definition 4: The Set Dependency Relation Model
To address this, we investigate a model representing the computer science state set $\mathbb{C}$ as isomorphic to the global sections of a continuous **cellular sheaf** ($\mathcal{F}$) over the finite physical cell complex $(\mathbb{P}, \le)$ [1.101, 1.102]. Cellular sheaves on cell complexes provide the precise mathematical gluing conditions required to compose local discrete physical evaluations into globally consistent states [1.97, 1.98, 1.101].

For each cell $\sigma \in \mathbb{P}$, the cellular sheaf assigns a stalk $\mathcal{F}(\sigma) = \mathbb{M} \times \mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}$ containing the mathematical and capability states [1.101]. For each face relation $\sigma \le \tau$, there exists a restriction map [1.101]:

$$
\mathcal{F}(\sigma \le \tau) : \mathcal{F}(\sigma) \longrightarrow \mathcal{F}(\tau)
$$

We define the global section space $\Gamma(\mathbb{P}, \mathcal{F})$ as the set of consistent configurations:

$$
\Gamma(\mathbb{P}, \mathcal{F}) = \left\{ s \in \prod_{\sigma \in \mathbb{P}} \mathcal{F}(\sigma) \;\middle|\; \forall \sigma \le \tau, \; \mathcal{F}(\sigma \le \tau)(s(\sigma)) = s(\tau) \land \psi_{\text{EFH}}(\sigma, s(\sigma)) = 1 \right\}
$$

Where:
*   $\psi_{\text{EFH}}$ is the **Invariance Characteristic Function [Empirical Horizon]** evaluated at each cell [1.97].

We investigate modeling the computer science execution state set $\mathbb{C}$ as strictly isomorphic to this space of global sections:

$$
\mathbb{C} \cong \Gamma(\mathbb{P}, \mathcal{F})
$$

### 3.3 Proposed Definition 5: An Exploratory Invariance Characteristic Function [Empirical Horizon]
The function $\psi_{\text{EFH}}(\sigma, m, l)$ is defined algebraically as [1.10]:

$$
\psi_{\text{EFH}}(\sigma, m, l) = \chi_{\text{spatial}}(\sigma, m) \land \chi_{\text{temporal}}(\sigma, l) \land \chi_{\text{parity}}(\sigma, m)
$$

Where:
*   **The Spatial Boundary Constraint ($\chi_{\text{spatial}}$):** Evaluates if the physical electrical potential $V(\sigma) \in \mathcal{V}^k_{\text{norm}}$ of cell $\sigma$ falls strictly within the mathematically defined topological boundaries $[L, R] \in m$ [1.26, 1.39]:

    $$
    \chi_{\text{spatial}}(\sigma, m) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } V(\sigma) \in [L, R] \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    $$

*   **The Temporal Capability Constraint ($\chi_{\text{temporal}}$):** Evaluates if the physical clock coordinate $t(\sigma) \in \mathcal{T}_{\text{norm}}$ falls strictly within the authorized logical epoch $[t_{\text{start}}, t_{\text{end}}] \in l$, grounded in trusted, hardware-timestamped secure enclaves to prevent temporal spoofing [1.52, 1.57]:

    $$
    \chi_{\text{temporal}}(\sigma, l) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } t(\sigma) \in [t_{\text{start}}, t_{\text{end}}] \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    $$

*   **The Hardware Parity Invariant ($\chi_{\text{parity}}$):** Evaluates the physical 3-bit Algebraic Parity/ECC of the cell state to verify that zero silicon-level bit corruption has occurred [1.26, 1.30]:

    $$
    \chi_{\text{parity}}(\sigma, m) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } \text{ECC}(V(\sigma)) = \text{Parity}(m) \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}

This cellular sheaf formulation is designed to evaluate the hypothesis that a state is globally consistent **if and only if** its local physical, mathematical, and logical coordinates cohere across all face inclusions in the simplicial grid [1.97, 1.98]. The cell constraints are evaluated discretely at the register-file write-back stage (per clock cycle), mapping the topological sheaf to a synchronous dataflow execution [1.15, 1.39].
