## Abstract

This paper explores a proposed mathematical bridge between the abstract global sections of the **Set Dependency Hypothesis [Empirical Horizon]** and the physical reality of silicon-level register files. Assuming the set-theoretic consistency of the cellular sheaf model ($\mathbb{C} \cong \Gamma(\mathbb{P}, \mathcal{F})$) formulated in `02.1` is true [1.97, 1.101], we investigate a formal mathematical mapping that defines how physical electrical potentials across discrete hardware cells can be translated constructively into the localized algebraic stalks of the sheaf. 

We introduce a proposed **Valuation Functor [Empirical Horizon]** designed to map continuous analog potentials to discrete dyadic rational sequences at a finite zoom horizon ($z \in \mathbb{N}$), establishing the exact mathematical boundary where physical hardware voltages generate computable numerical operands [1.18, 1.44]. By formalizing this mapping, we outline a proposed definition for the stalk $\mathcal{F}(\sigma)$—representing the mathematical source space of a Qn primitive—ensuring that every register-level valuation is structurally and inseparably bound to its local topological and object-capability constraints [1.10, 1.101]. This focused formulation establishes the necessary mathematical and physical linkage before proceeding to the formal algebraic axiomatizations of the primitives' exact arithmetic plane.

## Section 1: Introduction and Contextual Continuity

In our preceding paper `02.1` (v1.2.0), we formally investigated the **Set Dependency Hypothesis [Empirical Horizon]**, proposing a model where the computer science execution state set $\mathbb{C}$ is isomorphic to the global sections space $\Gamma(\mathbb{P}, \mathcal{F})$ of a cellular sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ over a discrete physical cell complex space $(\mathbb{P}, \le)$ [1.97, 1.101]:
$$
\mathbb{C} \cong \Gamma(\mathbb{P}, \mathcal{F} )
$$
Under this formulation, a global section $s \in \Gamma(\mathbb{P}, \mathcal{F})$ assigns to each discrete physical cell $\sigma \in \mathbb{P}$ a local section $s(\sigma)$ belonging to the stalk $\mathcal{F}(\sigma)$ of that cell [1.101]. This sheaf-theoretic representation was designed to evaluate the hypothesis that local, discrete hardware constraints (such as physical potential bounds and algebraic parity checks) must cohere globally across all face inclusions to form a valid, non-corrupted computation state [1.97, 1.98].

In sheaf theory, global sections are constructed by gluing together local sections defined over the individual **stalks** ($\mathcal{F}(\sigma)$) of the base cells [1.101]. If we cannot formally model how the physical hardware reality (the transistors, buses, and potentials on the silicon die) maps to these stalks, we face a major structural gap. The mathematical model remains an abstract ideal, disconnected from the material substrate.

Thus, this paper focuses strictly on the following hypothesis: **If the set dependency demonstration of `02.1` is true, then we can formally define the mathematical objects that map physical computer registers (cells $\sigma \in \mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}}$) to form the exact, constructive source space of a Qn primitive.**

## Section 2: The Discretized Register Cell Complex

To ground our algebraic models in physical silicon layout, we analyze the representation of the hardware register file as a finite, discrete cell complex [1.101, 1.102].

```
                     The Discretized Register Cell Complex
                     
      [ Cell σ_1 ] ───────[ Edge e_12 (Bus) ]───────> [ Cell σ_2 ]
    (Register Lane 1)                               (Register Lane 2)
```

### 2.1 Proposed Definition 1: The Finite Register Complex
Let $\mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}}$ be a finite, discrete cell complex (such as a simplicial complex or a discrete graph) representing the physical geometry of the silicon register file [1.101, 1.102]:
$$
\mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}} = (V_{\mathbb{P}}, E_{\mathbb{P}}, \mathcal{F}_{\mathbb{P}})
$$
Where:
*   A vertex $\sigma \in V_{\mathbb{P}}$ (a 0-cell) represents an isolated, local register storage lane or physical memory cell on the silicon die [1.102].
*   An edge $e \in E_{\mathbb{P}}$ (a 1-cell) represents a physical data bus or electrical propagation trace connecting two adjacent register lanes [1.39, 1.102].
*   A face $f \in \mathcal{F}_{\mathbb{P}}$ (a 2-cell) represents a localized arithmetic execution domain (such as a vector ALU lane) [1.39].

### 2.2 Proposed Definition 2: Dimensionless Potential Metrics on Register Cells
Under this discrete topological model, the physical state of any register lane $\sigma \in V_{\mathbb{P}}$ is represented by its local physical electrical potential $V(\sigma) \in \mathcal{V}^k_{\text{norm}}$, which is bounded by the physical capacitances and thermodynamic noise floors of the silicon junction [1.23, 1.39]. 

We define $\mathcal{V}^k_{\text{norm}}$ as the discrete potential set normalized by the characteristic junction thermal voltage $V_T$ to maintain absolute dimensional consistency across all discrete physical cells [1.39, 1.99]:
$$
V_T = \frac{k_{\text{B}} T}{q_{\text{e}}}
$$
where $k_{\text{B}}$ is Boltzmann's constant, $T$ is the temperature in Kelvin, and $q_{\text{e}}$ is the elementary charge [1.39].

## Section 3: The Valuation Mapping (Voltage-to-Rational Quantization)

Because the physical registers are fundamentally constrained to finite, discrete electrical states [1.39], we must define the mathematical operator that translates these continuous analog potentials into computable, constructive dyadic rationals [1.18, 1.44].

```
                        The Valuation Mapping (z = 4)
                        
   Analog Voltage V(σ) ────[ Valuation Functor Θ ]────> Discrete Rational (Φ(z) * 2^-z)
     (Continuous Potentials)                             (Computable Operands)
```

### 3.1 Proposed Definition 3: The Dyadic Rational Space
For any discrete zoom factor (precision index) $z \in \mathbb{N}$, we define the discrete dyadic rational space $\mathbb{Q}_{\text{dyadic}}^{(z)}$ as the set [1.44, 1.46]:
$$
\mathbb{Q}_{\text{dyadic}}^{(z)} = \left\{ a \cdot 2^{-z} \;\middle|\; a \in \mathbb{Z} \right\}
$$
where $2^{-z}$ represents the discrete resolution step of the active zoom horizon [1.44].

### 3.2 Proposed Definition 4: The Valuation Functor [Empirical Horizon]
We formally define the domain and codomain as categories to establish the rigorous, monotonic functorial properties of the mapping [1.35, 1.105]:
*   Let $\mathbf{V}_{\text{norm}}$ be the preorder category of normalized physical potentials, where the objects are potential distributions $\vec{v} \in \mathcal{V}^k_{\text{norm}}$ and morphisms represent physical potential inequalities ($\vec{v}_1 \le \vec{v}_2$) [1.39].
*   Let $\mathbf{Q}_{\text{dyadic}}^{(z)}$ be the poset category of dyadic rationals under the standard mathematical order ($\le$) [1.18].

We propose a covariant **Valuation Functor [Empirical Horizon]** $\Theta^{(z)}$ that maps between these categories, preserving the preorder inequalities to guarantee monotonic consistency across the analog-to-digital quantization boundary [1.18, 1.35, 1.105]:
$$
\Theta^{(z)} : \mathbf{V}_{\text{norm}} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Q}_{\text{dyadic}}^{(z)}
$$
$$
\Theta^{(z)}(V(\sigma)) = \Phi(z) \cdot 2^{-z}
$$
where $\Phi(z) \in \mathbb{Z}$ is the coarse, discrete integer quotient computed constructively via a physical hardware analog-to-digital (ADC) quantization pipeline [1.39, 1.44].

This valuation mapping represents the essential mathematical bridge between continuous physical potential and constructive rational operands. It is conjectured to ensure that the continuous analog "noise" of the silicon potentials is filtered into exact, discrete, and constructively decidable numerical values natively at the hardware-software boundary, preventing representation drift [1.18, 1.44].

## Section 4: Formulating the Stalk $\mathcal{F}(\sigma)$ (The Qn Source Space)

Using the valuation mapping, we formally define the algebraic stalk over any individual register cell $\sigma \in \mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}}$ [1.101]. This stalk behaves as the mathematically rigorous **source space** of a Qn primitive, proving that no quantitative value can exist in isolation from its safety boundaries and capability permissions [1.10, 1.50].

### 4.1 Proposed Definition 5: The Stalk of Local States
For any discrete register cell $\sigma \in \mathcal{K}_{\mathbb{P}}$, the stalk $\mathcal{F}(\sigma)$ of the cellular sheaf is defined as the algebraic product category [1.35, 1.101]:
$$
\mathcal{F}(\sigma) = \mathbb{M}^{(z)} \times \mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}(\sigma)
$$
We define the component spaces of this product category explicitly:
1.  **The Discrete Mathematical Base Space ($\mathbb{M}^{(z)}$):** The set [1.18, 1.44]
    $$
    \mathbb{M}^{(z)} = \mathbb{Q}_{\text{dyadic}}^{(z)} \times \text{Intervals}(\mathbb{R}_{\text{const}})
    $$
    representing the quantized quotient magnitude and its topologically closed boundary interval $[L, R]$, where $\text{Intervals}(\mathbb{R}_{\text{const}})$ is the set of closed intervals over constructive real numbers $\mathbb{R}_{\text{const}}$ [1.18].
2.  **The Local Intuitionistic Capability Space ($\mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}(\sigma)$):** The set [1.10, 1.57]
    $$
    \mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}(\sigma) = \mathcal{N} \times \mathcal{E}(\sigma)
    $$
    where $\mathcal{N}$ is the set of unforgeable 16-bit Semantic Namespace Tokens [1.10], and $\mathcal{E}(\sigma) = [t_{\text{start}}, t_{\text{end}}] \subset \mathcal{T}_{\text{norm}}$ is the local temporal validity epoch scaled by clock reference interval $\tau_0$ [1.77, 1.99].

We define $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$ as the canonical projection functors mapping the product category $\mathcal{F}(\sigma)$ to its respective components [1.35]:
$$
\pi_1 : \mathcal{F}(\sigma) \longrightarrow \mathbb{M}^{(z)}
$$
$$
\pi_2 : \mathcal{F}(\sigma) \longrightarrow \mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}(\sigma)
$$

### 4.2 Proposed Definition 6: The Stalk-Level Invariance Characteristic Function [Empirical Horizon]
We define the local Invariance Characteristic Function $\psi_{\text{EFH}}$ over cell $\sigma$ as the map [1.10, 1.97]:
$$
\psi_{\text{EFH}} : V_{\mathbb{P}} \times \mathbb{M}^{(z)} \times \mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}(\sigma) \longrightarrow \{0, 1\}
$$
$$
\psi_{\text{EFH}}(\sigma, m, l) = \chi_{\text{spatial}}(\sigma, m) \land \chi_{\text{temporal}}(\sigma, l) \land \chi_{\text{parity}}(\sigma, m)
$$
We define each characteristic sub-function explicitly to prevent un-introduced operations:
*   **The Spatial Check ($\chi_{\text{spatial}}$):** Evaluates as [1.26, 1.39]
    $$
    \chi_{\text{spatial}}(\sigma, m) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } V(\sigma) \in \pi_{\text{interval}}(m) \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    $$
    where $\pi_{\text{interval}} : \mathbb{M}^{(z)} \to \text{Intervals}(\mathbb{R}_{\text{const}})$ is the projection mapping $m \in \mathbb{M}^{(z)}$ to its interval component $[L, R]$ [1.18].
*   **The Temporal Check ($\chi_{\text{temporal}}$):** Evaluates as [1.57]
    $$
    \chi_{\text{temporal}}(\sigma, l) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } t(\sigma) \in \pi_{\text{epoch}}(l) \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    $$
    where $t(\sigma) \in \mathcal{T}_{\text{norm}}$ is the physical clock state of cell $\sigma$, and $\pi_{\text{epoch}} : \mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}(\sigma) \to \mathcal{E}(\sigma)$ is the projection mapping $l \in \mathbf{L}_{\text{logic}}(\sigma)$ to its epoch component $[t_{\text{start}}, t_{\text{end}}]$ [1.10].
*   **The Parity Check ($\chi_{\text{parity}}$):** Evaluates as [1.26, 1.30]
    $$
    \chi_{\text{parity}}(\sigma, m) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } \text{ECC}(V(\sigma)) = \text{Parity}(m) \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    $$
    where $\text{ECC} : \mathcal{V}^k_{\text{norm}} \to \{0, 1\}^3$ is the physical 3-bit algebraic parity generator [1.26], and $\text{Parity} : \mathbb{M}^{(z)} \to \{0, 1\}^3$ is the mathematical parity mapped out-of-band [1.26].

By formulating the stalk $\mathcal{F}(\sigma)$ as a product category and defining the invariance checks explicitly, we model the hypothesis that the quantitative value, the topological spatial bounds, the namespace permissions, and the temporal epoch are **inseparable components of a single, unified mathematical object** [1.10, 1.50]. 

This proposed structure ensures that any arithmetic state transition executed on cell $\sigma$ is evaluated in parallel against its local capabilities and constraints, mathematically preventing the logical punctures that characterize the Semantic Void of traditional register architectures [1.2, 1.10].
