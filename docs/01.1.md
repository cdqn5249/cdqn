# Axiomatic Foundations of Path-Theoretic Semirings: The Genesis of the Quang Number ($Q_n$)

## Abstract

The contemporary computational landscape is rapidly approaching a terminal entropy wall. This boundary is driven by the fundamental decoupling of numerical magnitude from semantic intent. As decentralized networks and artificial intelligence systems scale to global proportions, the reliance on the IEEE 754 floating-point standard and the traditional von Neumann execution architecture has exposed critical physical and mathematical vulnerabilities. These vulnerabilities manifest as operational non-determinism in parallel associative environments, prohibitive energy costs tied to data movement rather than arithmetic execution, and the infeasibility of deterministic distributed consensus across heterogeneous hardware configurations. 

This foundational specification introduces the **Quang Number ($Q_n$)**, a novel numerical framework explicitly engineered to achieve **Minimum Possible Latency** and **Intent-Deterministic Execution**. By transitioning computational philosophy away from Platonic abstraction toward a paradigm of **Constructivist Physicalism**[† Empirical Horizon], the $Q_n$ framework redefines a number. It is no longer an abstract scalar magnitude to be loaded into a register; rather, it is a deterministic, finite trajectory—a coordinate path—navigated on a quasicrystalline, aperiodic manifold. 

We formalize this systemic shift algebraically as a **Path-Theoretic Semiring** ($Q$-Semiring) [† Empirical Horizon]. This mathematical structure utilizes established theorems in shortest-distance graph algorithms, weighted automata, and hyperdimensional computing to map discrete calculations to geometric spatial shifts. Through this geometric reconfiguration, the $Q_n$ system introduces **Ontological Gating ($Q_\Omega$)** [† Empirical Horizon] and hardware-level **Morphism Gates** [† Empirical Horizon]. These mechanisms fuse the data type, the semantic meaning, and the execution constraints directly into the hardware bit-pattern, effectively eliminating the energy-intensive Fetch-Decode-Execute cycle for semantic intent parsing. The resulting **Time-Gain** metric [† Empirical Horizon] offers a scalable, absolute deterministic, and physically sovereign substrate for the next generation of chained and distributed intelligence.

## 1. Introduction: The Entropy Crisis in Magnitude-Based Computing

For over seven decades, computer science has relied on the abstraction of numbers as scalar magnitudes. This paradigm, explicitly formalized in silicon hardware via fixed-width integer formats and the ubiquitous IEEE 754 floating-point standard, perfectly served the era of centralized, homogeneous, and sequential processing. However, in the context of massively parallel, heterogeneous distributed intelligence—where millions of disparate nodes must maintain perfect consensus regarding high-dimensional data—treating numbers as abstract magnitudes wholly separated from their semantic context has become the primary source of systemic computational entropy.

### 1.1 The Illusion of Floating-Point Determinism in Distributed Reductions

The foundational assumption that classical floating-point mathematics provides a stable substrate for distributed consensus is empirically false. Because floating-point arithmetic operates on finite, rounded mantissa representations of real numbers, it fundamentally violates the associative property of addition and multiplication. In strict mathematical terms, for floating-point values $a, b,$ and $c$, it is routinely true that $(a \oplus b) \oplus c \neq a \oplus (b \oplus c)$. In parallel computing environments, the arbitrary order of thread execution and network-delayed reductions dictates the accumulation of rounding errors, leading to divergent bit-level results across different nodes calculating the exact same formula.

*   **Empirical Source 1.1.1:** In their comprehensive analysis of reproducibility in high-performance computing (HPC), Zhou et al. (2023) documented how parallel computation introduces out-of-order floating-point arithmetic, which systematically destroys results replicability. As noted in their survey on *Reproducibility, Replicability, and Repeatability*, "minor precision errors owing to the lack of associativity can quickly influence large-scale computing, which involves billions of operations." The non-determinism inherent in distributed reductions makes bit-for-bit consensus on legacy floating-point values architecturally impossible without forcing sequential, single-threaded bottlenecks [1].
*   **Empirical Source 1.1.2:** The mathematical difficulty of resolving distributed numeric constraints is further formally proven by Aanjaneya and Nagarakatte (Rutgers University, 2024). In their PLDI paper, *Maximum Consensus Floating Point Solutions for Infeasible Low-Dimensional Linear Programs*, they demonstrate that finding a maximum consensus floating-point solution in systems with billions of overlapping, infeasible constraints is an NP-hard geometric problem. They show that floating-point fragility limits network-wide scalability when subjected to high-dimensional constraints [2].

### 1.2 The Von Neumann Energy and Memory Wall

Beyond the mathematical fragility of IEEE 754, the physical execution of magnitude-based computing is profoundly inefficient due to the von Neumann bottleneck—the strict physical and logical separation of memory and processing. When numbers are "blind" to their semantic meaning, the CPU must expend vast amounts of thermodynamic energy fetching instructions from memory simply to understand *what* rule set to apply to the numerical payload.

*   **Empirical Source 1.2.1:** The foundational physical floor for this critique was established by Mark Horowitz's seminal ISSCC paper, *Computing's Energy Problem* (2014). Horowitz demonstrated that the energy cost of performing a base arithmetic operation (e.g., $\approx 1$ picojoule (pJ) for a 64-bit ALU addition) is negligible compared to the energy required to fetch the instruction and move the data (often $\approx 70$ pJ per instruction fetch from cache, scaling to several nanojoules for off-chip DRAM access) [3].
*   **Empirical Source 1.2.2:** This thermodynamic constraint was anticipated by Wulf and McKee (1995) in their definition of the "Memory Wall," which posited that processor speed increases would inevitably outpace memory access times, bottlenecking all computation at the data-movement layer rather than the logic layer [4].
*   **Empirical Source 1.2.3:** Keckler et al. (2011) confirmed this trajectory in *GPUs and the future of parallel computing*, projecting that by the exascale era, data movement would consume the entire thermal power budget of a processor if the von Neumann separation of instruction, address, and scalar value was not fundamentally reformed [5].

By decoupling the "meaning" (the instruction) from the "value" (the number), legacy systems waste the overwhelming majority of their power budget on routing and fetching. To gain compute time and drastically reduce thermodynamic energy profiles, the instruction, the location, and the value must be fused into a single, self-describing geometric entity.

## 2. Constructivist Physicalism and Semantic Binding

To resolve the dual crises of non-determinism (Sources 1.1.1, 1.1.2) and energy-wasting data movement (Sources 1.2.1, 1.2.2, 1.2.3), the Chained and Distributed Quang Network (CDQN) proposes a fundamental shift in computational ontology: **Constructivist Physicalism** [† Empirical Horizon].

This paradigm asserts that a number does not "exist" as a Platonic ideal in an abstract mathematical vacuum. Instead, a number is **Manifested** by a physical machine for a specific, intent-driven task. Under this axiom, uncomputable infinities and abstract magnitudes are discarded in favor of geometrically finite execution paths.

### 2.1 The Number as a Coordinate Trajectory

If the goal is to eliminate instruction-fetch overhead and guarantee absolute determinism across a heterogeneous network, we must cease treating numbers as abstract objects manipulated by external, fetch-dependent software algorithms. Instead, the number must be defined as a **Coordinate Trajectory** on a pre-defined topological manifold. 

*   **Legacy Model (Algebraic Magnitude):** `Load Instruction` $\to$ `Load Value A` $\to$ `Load Value B` $\to$ `ALU Calculate` $\to$ `Store Result`. (High latency, high energy, non-deterministic rounding).
*   **Quang Model (Geometric Navigation) [† Empirical Horizon]:** `State A` $\to$ `Shift to State B via Geodesic Path`. (Zero instruction-fetch latency, minimal energy, exact geometric determinism).

### 2.2 Hyperdimensional Semantic Integration

To embed semantic meaning (the instruction and intent) directly into the number itself, the $Q_n$ framework draws upon the proven mathematics of Vector Symbolic Architectures (VSA) and Hyperdimensional Computing (HDC).

*   **Empirical Source 2.2.1:** Kanerva’s foundational framework, *Hyperdimensional Computing* (2009), proves that high-dimensional spaces (e.g., $D=10,000$) provide a robust, distributed representation of semantics. In HDC, concepts, relations, and scalar values are all represented by wide bit-vectors. Operations like "Binding" (typically XOR) and "Bundling" (majority-rule addition) distribute meaning holistically across the entire coordinate, allowing the coordinate itself to represent complex, composite concepts without external lookup tables [6].
*   **Empirical Source 2.2.2:** Gayler (2003) demonstrated in *Vector Symbolic Architectures* that systematic binding operations can encode structural hierarchies natively within vector algebra, allowing machines to inherently "understand" grammatical and semantic structures purely through vector proximity [7].
*   **Empirical Source 2.2.3:** Rachkovskij et al. (2005) further proved that distributed representations allow for structure mapping in semantic networks, where analogical reasoning is reduced to a computationally inexpensive measurement of geometric overlap [8].

The Quang Number specification extends these HDC principles by transitioning from probabilistic, random high-dimensional vectors to deterministic, structured paths on aperiodic manifolds. The user's "Intent" is intrinsically bound to the topological address of the coordinate, eliminating the need for software-level semantic inference entirely.

## 3. Algebraic Foundations: The $Q$-Semiring [† Empirical Horizon]

Because a $Q_n$ is defined as a trajectory (a path) through a directed manifold rather than a static scalar magnitude, it cannot obey the axioms of a classical mathematical field (such as the field of real numbers $\mathbb{R}$). A mathematical field strictly requires that every element possess a unique additive inverse (subtraction) and multiplicative inverse (division) that perfectly returns the system to an absolute zero origin. In directed graphs and path-theoretic aperiodic structures, reverse traversal (deflation) is not algebraically symmetric to forward inflationary growth; it does not always yield a single, unique predecessor path.

Therefore, the algebraic foundation of the Quang Number system is formally classified as a **Path-Theoretic Semiring** ($Q$-Semiring).

### 3.1 The Semiring Framework for Path Operations

A semiring $(\mathcal{Q}, \oplus, \otimes, \bar{0}, \bar{1})$ is an algebraic structure equipped with two primary operations where addition is commutative and associative, multiplication is associative and distributes over addition, but elements are not strictly required to have unique additive inverses.

*   **Empirical Source 3.1.1:** Mohri (2002) in *Semiring Frameworks and Algorithms for Shortest-Distance Problems* formally proved that semirings are the optimal algebraic structure for defining shortest-path and network navigation algorithms. By defining "addition" as path-union (or cost minimization) and "multiplication" as path-extension, Mohri demonstrated that complex graph-state problems reduce to highly efficient, generalized algebraic operations [9].
*   **Empirical Source 3.1.2:** Golan (2013) in *Semirings and their Applications* extensively documents how semirings model automata theory and dynamic programming on graphs without requiring the strict inverse laws of rings or fields [10].
*   **Empirical Source 3.1.3:** Droste et al. (2009) in the *Handbook of Weighted Automata* prove that weighted automata over semirings allow for the deterministic computation of behavior in systems where paths carry semantic or probabilistic "weights" [11].

### 3.2 The Substitution-Transition Lemma [† Empirical Horizon]

To explicitly map Mohri's generalized shortest-distance semiring to the specific geometry of the $Q_n$ framework, we introduce the **Substitution-Transition Lemma**.

Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite alphabet of geometric prototiles. A substitution rule $\sigma: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{A}^+$ generates the aperiodic manifold $\mathcal{M}$. A Quang Number path $\omega$ is defined as a specific deterministic word generated within $\mathcal{A}^*$.

1.  **Additive Monoid ($\oplus$):** Represents the **Canonical Geodesic Union**. If an actant attempts to traverse path $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$, the $\oplus$ operator identifies the canonical shortest word (geodesic) connecting the origin to the combined terminal state in the localized graph topology. The identity element $\bar{0}$ is the Genesis Tile (`@0_0`).
2.  **Multiplicative Monoid ($\otimes$):** Represents **Fractal Substitution Composition**. The operation $\omega_1 \otimes \omega_2$ maps the trajectory via the hierarchical inflation rule $\sigma$. Every primitive geometric step in $\omega_1$ is replaced by the macro-structure of $\omega_2$. The identity element $\bar{1}$ is the fundamental unit substitution rule.
3.  **Annihilation:** The Genesis Tile $\bar{0}$ acts as the strict annihilator under multiplication: $\omega \otimes \bar{0} = \bar{0} \otimes \omega = \bar{0}$.

**Lemma:** *The aperiodic substitution graph forms a complete semiring $(\mathcal{Q}, \oplus, \otimes, \bar{0}, \bar{1})$ where the associative and distributive properties of $\otimes$ over $\oplus$ are mathematically guaranteed by the strictly hierarchical nature of the inflation rules, as super-tiles uniquely and deterministically decompose into sub-tiles without topological ambiguity.*

## 4. The Geometric Substrate: Aperiodic DAG-Manifolds

For the $Q$-Semiring to function deterministically at a global scale, the coordinate space—the substrate on which paths are navigated—must possess distinct topological properties. It must allow for infinite scaling without coordinate collision, and it must support local symmetry rules without yielding to global periodicity.

The $Q_n$ substrate is defined as a **Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)** projected onto an **Aperiodic Manifold** (specifically utilizing Amman-Beenker 8-fold symmetry or generalized Penrose 5-fold tiling paradigms).

### 4.1 The Necessity of Aperiodic Order

If the coordinate substrate were a standard, periodic Euclidean grid (e.g., an $X, Y, Z$ Cartesian array), the system would inevitably require a centralized, global registry counter to prevent two entirely different semantic intents from being assigned the same coordinate via modulo wrap-around. Furthermore, periodic grids lack the asymmetrical "Lock-and-Key" geometric diversity required to enforce type safety natively at the hardware level.

Aperiodic tilings solve this by exhibiting perfect long-range order while ensuring that no local configuration of tiles ever repeats exactly across the infinite plane.

*   **Empirical Source 4.1.1:** Baake and Grimm (2013), in their authoritative text *Aperiodic Order: A Mathematical Invitation*, formalize the physics and mathematics of quasicrystalline structures. They prove that substitution rules applied to a finite set of prototiles generate infinite, deterministic, non-periodic lattices. Because the configuration of tiles around any given coordinate is globally unique, the coordinate's localized geometry inherently serves as a robust, non-colliding address [12].

### 4.2 Address Collision Bounds and Inflation Limits

The probability of address collision approaches exactly zero as the manifold generation $G \to \infty$. In a strictly periodic lattice, there exists a translational symmetry vector $v \neq 0$ such that the topology $T + v = T$. In an Amman-Beenker or Penrose aperiodic tiling, no such translational vector exists. 

Consequently, any two paths $\omega_1 \neq \omega_2$ originating from the Genesis Tile $\bar{0}$ will map to unique terminal coordinates with a collision probability bound strictly at $P(C) = 0$.

*   **Multiplication as Inflation:** Aperiodic tilings are self-similar across macro-scales. A set of sub-tiles groups into a "Super-Tile" that obeys the exact geometric matching rules of the original base tiles. This mathematical property, **Inflation**, enables the $Q_n$ system to scale a numerical value infinitely upwards without experiencing the "bit-overflow" integer limits of standard 64-bit ALUs.

2.  **World 1 (Logical `l.`):** The zone of arithmetic consistency, smart contracts, and computational protocols. Operations here utilize the base semiring *plus* **TRS-L** (Structural Invariance). It demands absolute algebraic closure and dependency tracking, but does not require physical actuation.
