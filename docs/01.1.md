# Axiomatic Foundations of Path-Theoretic Semirings: The Genesis of the Quang Number ($Q_n$)

## Abstract

The contemporary computational landscape is rapidly approaching a terminal entropy wall driven by the fundamental decoupling of numerical magnitude from semantic intent. As decentralized networks and artificial intelligence systems scale, the reliance on the IEEE 754 floating-point standard and traditional von Neumann execution architectures has exposed critical physical and mathematical vulnerabilities. These manifest as operational non-determinism in parallel environments, prohibitive energy costs tied to data movement rather than execution, and rigid taxonomies unable to handle emergent human creativity.

This foundational specification introduces the **Quang Number ($Q_n$)**, a novel numerical framework explicitly engineered to achieve **Minimum Possible Latency** and **Intent-Deterministic Execution** across all forms of computational labor. By transitioning computational philosophy toward **Constructivist Physicalism** [† Empirical Horizon], the $Q_n$ framework redefines a number not as an abstract scalar, but as a deterministic, finite trajectory on a quasicrystalline, aperiodic manifold. 

We formalize this systemic shift algebraically as a **Path-Theoretic Semiring** ($Q$-Semiring) [† Empirical Horizon]. Through this geometric reconfiguration, the $Q_n$ system introduces **Ontological Gating ($Q_\Omega$)** and **Dynamic Domains ($Q_D$)** [† Empirical Horizon], establishing an orthogonal relationship between physical execution constraints and semantic vocabularies. This allows the hardware to seamlessly process rigid scientific consensus alongside emergent artistic domains using the exact same thermodynamic substrate. The resulting **Time-Gain** metric [† Empirical Horizon] offers a scalable, absolute deterministic, and physically sovereign foundation for the next generation of chained and distributed intelligence.

## 1. Introduction: The Entropy Crisis in Magnitude-Based Computing

For over seven decades, computer science has relied on the abstraction of numbers as scalar magnitudes. This paradigm, formalized in silicon via fixed-width integer formats and the IEEE 754 floating-point standard, served the era of centralized, homogeneous processing perfectly. However, in the context of massively parallel distributed intelligence—where millions of nodes must maintain perfect consensus regarding high-dimensional data—treating numbers as abstract magnitudes wholly separated from their semantic context has become the primary source of systemic computational entropy.

### 1.1 The Illusion of Floating-Point Determinism in Distributed Reductions

Because floating-point arithmetic operates on finite, rounded mantissa representations of real numbers, it fundamentally violates the associative property of addition and multiplication[$(a \oplus b) \oplus c \neq a \oplus (b \oplus c)$]. In parallel computing environments, the arbitrary order of thread execution dictates the accumulation of rounding errors, leading to divergent bit-level results across different nodes calculating the exact same formula.

*   **Empirical Source 1.1.1:** Zhou et al. (2023) documented how parallel computation introduces out-of-order floating-point arithmetic, systematically destroying results replicability. As noted in their survey *Reproducibility, Replicability, and Repeatability*, "minor precision errors owing to the lack of associativity can quickly influence large-scale computing." The non-determinism inherent in distributed reductions makes bit-for-bit consensus on legacy floating-point values architecturally impossible without forcing sequential bottlenecks [1].
*   **Empirical Source 1.1.2:** Aanjaneya and Nagarakatte (Rutgers University, 2024) formally proved in *Maximum Consensus Floating Point Solutions for Infeasible Low-Dimensional Linear Programs* that finding a maximum consensus floating-point solution in systems with billions of overlapping constraints is an NP-hard geometric problem, severely limiting network-wide scalability [2].

### 1.2 The Von Neumann Energy and Memory Wall

The physical execution of magnitude-based computing is profoundly inefficient due to the strict separation of memory and processing. When numbers are "blind" to their semantic meaning, the CPU must expend vast amounts of thermodynamic energy fetching instructions from memory simply to understand *what* rule set to apply to the numerical payload.

*   **Empirical Source 1.2.1:** Mark Horowitz's seminal ISSCC paper, *Computing's Energy Problem* (2014), established that the energy cost of performing a base arithmetic operation ($\approx 1$ pJ for a 64-bit ALU addition) is negligible compared to the energy required to fetch the instruction and move the data (often $\approx 70$ pJ per instruction fetch) [3].
*   **Empirical Source 1.2.2:** Wulf and McKee (1995) defined this as the "Memory Wall," positing that processor speed increases inevitably outpace memory access times, bottlenecking all computation at the data-movement layer rather than the logic layer [4].
*   **Empirical Source 1.2.3:** Keckler et al. (2011) confirmed this trajectory in *GPUs and the future of parallel computing*, projecting that data movement would consume the entire thermal power budget of a processor if the separation of instruction, address, and scalar value was not fundamentally reformed [5].

To gain compute time and drastically reduce thermodynamic energy profiles, the instruction, the location, and the value must be fused into a single, self-describing geometric entity.

## 2. Constructivist Physicalism and Semantic Binding

To resolve these crises, the Chained and Distributed Quang Network (CDQN) proposes a fundamental shift in computational ontology: **Constructivist Physicalism**[† Empirical Horizon].

This paradigm asserts that a number does not "exist" as a Platonic ideal in an abstract mathematical vacuum. Instead, a number is **Manifested** by a physical machine for a specific, intent-driven task. Under this axiom, uncomputable infinities are discarded in favor of geometrically finite execution paths.

### 2.1 The Number as a Coordinate Trajectory

If the goal is to eliminate instruction-fetch overhead, we must cease treating numbers as abstract objects manipulated by external algorithms. Instead, the number must be defined as a **Coordinate Trajectory** on a pre-defined topological manifold. This applies equally to all forms of computational labor: resolving a cryptographic hash requires the exact same fundamental thermodynamic navigation as rendering an interactive entertainment environment.

*   **Legacy Model:** `Load Instruction` $\to$ `Load Value A` $\to$ `Load Value B` $\to$ `ALU Calculate` $\to$ `Store Result`.
*   **Quang Model [† Empirical Horizon]:** `State A` $\to$ `Shift to State B via Geodesic Path`. (Zero instruction-fetch latency, minimal energy).

### 2.2 Hyperdimensional Semantic Integration

To embed semantic meaning (the instruction and intent) directly into the number itself, the $Q_n$ framework draws upon Vector Symbolic Architectures (VSA) and Hyperdimensional Computing (HDC).

*   **Empirical Source 2.2.1:** Kanerva (2009) proves in *Hyperdimensional Computing* that high-dimensional spaces provide a robust, distributed representation of semantics. Operations like "Binding" (XOR) and "Bundling" (majority addition) distribute meaning holistically across the coordinate, allowing the coordinate itself to represent complex, composite concepts without lookup tables [6].
*   **Empirical Source 2.2.2:** Gayler (2003) demonstrated that systematic binding operations encode structural hierarchies natively within vector algebra, allowing machines to inherently map semantic and grammatical structures through vector proximity [7].
*   **Empirical Source 2.2.3:** Rachkovskij et al. (2005) further proved that distributed representations allow for structure mapping in semantic networks, reducing analogical reasoning to computationally inexpensive measurements of geometric overlap [8].

## 3. Algebraic Foundations: The $Q$-Semiring [† Empirical Horizon]

Because a $Q_n$ is defined as a directed trajectory rather than a static scalar, it cannot obey the axioms of a classical mathematical field (which requires perfect additive and multiplicative inverses). In directed graphs and aperiodic structures, reverse traversal (deflation) is not algebraically symmetric to forward inflationary growth.

Therefore, the algebraic foundation of the Quang Number system is formally classified as a **Path-Theoretic Semiring** ($Q$-Semiring).

### 3.1 The Semiring Framework for Path Operations

A semiring $(\mathcal{Q}, \oplus, \otimes, \bar{0}, \bar{1})$ is an algebraic structure where addition is commutative and associative, multiplication distributes over addition, but elements are not strictly required to have unique additive inverses.

*   **Empirical Source 3.1.1:** Mohri (2002) formally proved that semirings are the optimal algebraic structure for shortest-distance and network navigation algorithms. Defining "addition" as path-union and "multiplication" as path-extension reduces complex graph-state problems to highly efficient generalized algebraic operations [9].
*   **Empirical Source 3.1.2:** Golan (1999) extensively documents how semirings model automata theory and dynamic programming on graphs without requiring the strict inverse laws of rings or fields [10].
*   **Empirical Source 3.1.3:** Droste, Kuich, and Vogler (2009) prove in the *Handbook of Weighted Automata* that weighted automata over semirings allow for deterministic computation in systems where paths carry semantic or probabilistic constraints [11].

### 3.2 The Substitution-Transition Lemma [† Empirical Horizon]

To explicitly map Mohri's shortest-distance semiring to the $Q_n$ framework, we introduce the **Substitution-Transition Lemma**. Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite alphabet of geometric prototiles, and $\sigma: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{A}^+$ be a substitution rule generating an aperiodic manifold.

1.  **Additive Monoid ($\oplus$):** Represents **Canonical Geodesic Union**. The $\oplus$ operator identifies the shortest word connecting the origin to the combined terminal state. Identity $\bar{0}$ is the Genesis Tile (`@0_0`).
2.  **Multiplicative Monoid ($\otimes$):** Represents **Fractal Substitution Composition**. $\omega_1 \otimes \omega_2$ maps the trajectory via the hierarchical inflation rule $\sigma$. Every primitive geometric step in $\omega_1$ is replaced by the macro-structure of $\omega_2$. Identity $\bar{1}$ is the unit substitution rule.
3.  **Annihilation:** The Genesis Tile acts as the strict annihilator: $\omega \otimes \bar{0} = \bar{0} \otimes \omega = \bar{0}$.

## 4. The Geometric Substrate: Aperiodic DAG-Manifolds

For the $Q$-Semiring to function deterministically at scale, the coordinate space must allow infinite scaling without collision and support local symmetry without global periodicity. The $Q_n$ substrate is defined as a **Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)** projected onto an **Aperiodic Manifold**.

### 4.1 The Necessity of Aperiodic Order

Periodic grids (like Cartesian arrays) inevitably require centralized global registries to prevent semantic intents from colliding via modulo wrap-around. Furthermore, periodic grids lack the asymmetrical "Lock-and-Key" geometric diversity required to enforce type safety natively at the hardware level.

*   **Empirical Source 4.1.1:** Baake and Grimm (2013) formalize the mathematics of quasicrystalline structures, proving that substitution rules applied to prototiles generate infinite, deterministic, non-periodic lattices. Because the configuration of tiles around any given coordinate is globally unique, the localized geometry inherently serves as a robust, non-colliding address [12].

Any two paths $\omega_1 \neq \omega_2$ originating from the Genesis Tile $\bar{0}$ will map to unique terminal coordinates with a collision probability bound strictly at $P(C) = 0$.

## 5. Ontological Gating and Dynamic Domains [† Empirical Horizon]

The paradigm-shifting capability of the Quang Number is its ability to bind human semantic intent securely to geometric mathematics. To achieve this logically and consistently, the architecture defines an **orthogonal relationship** between **Ontological Worlds ($Q_\Omega$)** and **Dynamic Domains ($Q_D$)**.

### 5.1 The Orthogonality of Worlds and Domains

In previous iterations of legacy computing, semantic context is lost at the ALU. In the $Q_n$ framework, coordinate trajectories are explicitly bound by two meta-operators that serve separate but composing functions.

#### 5.1.1 The Three Ontological Worlds ($Q_\Omega$)
The Worlds act as Functorial Lenses defining the *feasibility and actuation constraints* of the manifold traversal:
1.  **World 0 (Physical `p.`):** The zone of hardware actuation and physical reality. Constrained by Newtonian limits (TRS-N) and requires thermodynamic proof of presence (silicon entropy) to execute.
2.  **World 1 (Logical `l.`):** The zone of arithmetic consistency and computational protocols. Constrained by strict algebraic rules of the $Q$-Semiring (TRS-L).
3.  **World 2 (Narrative `n.`):** The zone of abstract potential, hypothesis, and simulation. Constrained only by local topological connectivity (TRS-P).

#### 5.1.2 Dynamic Nomenclatures and Emergent Domains ($Q_D$)
While the Worlds ($Q_\Omega$) govern the *physics of execution*, the Domains ($Q_D$) govern the *semantic vocabulary and relational geometry*. Crucially, Domains are not a rigid, top-down scientific taxonomy, nor are they restricted to specific Worlds. They are **Dynamic and Composable Semantic Sub-Graphs**.

*   **Empirical Source 5.1.1:** Franceschelli and Musolesi (2024) in *Creativity and Machine Learning: A Survey* establish that computational creativity requires evaluating outputs across multiple constraints simultaneously, such as value (logical utility) and novelty (narrative divergence). The generation of artifacts like music or art profoundly diverges from simple data-fetching; it is a multi-constraint structural mapping problem [13].

In the $Q_n$ framework, an emergent artistic domain like `mus` (Music) is a hybrid lattice. It does not exist solely in the Narrative World. Because Domains and Worlds are orthogonal, a user can instantiate:
*   **`p.mus.C_major`**: The physical actuation of acoustic waves via hardware speakers.
*   **`l.mus.C_major`**: The logical, mathematical verification of harmonic 4:5:6 frequency ratios.
*   **`n.mus.C_major`**: The generative, algorithmic hypothesis of a new scale.

Because the hardware treats the thermodynamic execution of a scientific simulation and an artistic rendering as the exact same process (Geometric Pathfinding), these domains seamlessly compose. The system allows true Universal Turing-Complete expression without risking "Logic Leakage."

### 5.2 Morphism Gates and Silicon Type-Safety [† Empirical Horizon]

By integrating the $Q_\Omega$ and $Q_D$ indices directly into the prefix bits of the Quang register, the hardware applies **Morphism Gates** in a single clock cycle.
