### Glossary of Terms

* **CDQN System:** **[Empirical Horizon]** (Chained and Distributed Quang Numbers). A deterministic computational framework envisioned to function as a **Harness of Variation, Evaluation, and Selection (VES)**. It is designed to enable semantic computation on standard hardware without relying on stochastic or autonomous AI models.
* **Contextual Zoom ($z$):** **[Empirical Horizon]** An integer-based scaling mechanism intended to replace floating-point arithmetic. It applies a decimal shift operator ($M \times 10^{-z}$) to a fixed-point magnitude, aiming to preserve absolute precision without hardware overflow.
* **Proof of Birth (PoB):** **[Scientific Consensus / Hardware]** A cryptographic metadata tag utilizing Physical Unclonable Functions (PUFs)—such as localized thermal drift—to anchor a digital state to its chronological creation point in physical silicon.
* **Quadripartite Matrix:** **[Empirical Horizon]** The proposed foundational 64-Byte structure of a Qn, unifying four categorical domains: Maths ($M$), Semantics ($S$), Operations ($Op$), and Constraints ($Halt$).
* **Quang:** A Vietnamese concept translating to *clarity, brightness, and integrity*. It serves as the foundational design philosophy aiming to ensure systemic transparency, affordability, and determinism.
* **Semantic Hypervector:** **[Scientific Consensus]** A high-dimensional binary array (derived from Vector Symbolic Architectures) capable of storing conceptual relationships and Markov Chain dependencies via distributed superposition, evaluated via bitwise operations.
* **Zero-Compute Bypass:** **[Empirical Horizon]** Proposed hardware-level topological routing instructions. Designed to intercept mathematically undefined or static operations (e.g., division by zero) and return geometric reflections, with the goal of bypassing Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) iterative execution.

### Abstract

Modern computational architectures face compounding challenges regarding energy efficiency, memory bandwidth, and deterministic semantic processing. The current reliance on probabilistic Large Language Models (LLMs) requires substantial, centralized hardware clusters, creating a barrier to localized, sovereign computation. Furthermore, legacy data structures are known to exacerbate the Von Neumann bottleneck through intensive memory serialization and floating-point exception handling. This paper introduces the theoretical foundation of the **Quang Number (Qn)** primitive: a hardware-aligned, 64-byte computational unit designed to natively process semantic topology. By unifying magnitude, semantic vectors, operational routing, and cryptographic bounds into a single, L1 Cache-aligned matrix, the Qn framework seeks to maximize affordability and deployment on consumer-grade Systems-on-a-Chip (SoCs). The Qn primitive is proposed as the foundation for the CDQN System, a deterministic harness structured around Variation, Evaluation, and Selection. This theoretical architecture pursues four primary technical objectives: Efficiency (hardware alignment), Performance (serialization mitigation), Modularity (deterministic semantics), and Security (physical provenance).

### 1. Introduction: Affordability and the Deterministic Harness

The evolution of contemporary computer science has heavily prioritized abstraction and emergent capability, culminating in the widespread adoption of stochastic neural networks. While these models effectively approximate natural language processing, their reliance on billion-parameter matrices necessitates centralized, high-energy hardware infrastructure, limiting the deployment of advanced semantic computation on edge devices.

#### 1.1 The Centralization of Semantic Computation
**[Scientific Consensus]** The computational limits of deep learning present severe economic and environmental constraints. The dependency on centralized GPU clusters creates a computational monopoly, while the probabilistic nature of LLMs prevents deterministic guarantees of accuracy. 
* **Source 1:** *Thompson et al. (2020), "The Computational Limits of Deep Learning."* Demonstrates that the computational power required to sustain deep learning performance improvements is scaling at an unsustainable, exponentially cost-prohibitive rate.
* **Source 2:** *Bender et al. (2021), "On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots."* Outlines the inherent limitations of massive language models, specifically their inability to ground text in objective semantic meaning without generating probabilistic output.

#### 1.2 The Derived Claim: The CDQN VES Harness
**[Empirical Horizon]** To ensure equitable access to high-level semantic tools, computation must ideally be executable on affordable, commodity silicon (e.g., consumer-grade processors and localized storage). Achieving this requires exploring a shift from probabilistic inference to deterministic calculation.

The proposed theoretical solution is the **Quang Number (Qn)** primitive, intended to be utilized within the **CDQN System**. The CDQN is envisioned not as an autonomous artificial intelligence, but as a computational harness governed by three deterministic principles:
1. **Variation:** The ability to generate mathematical combinations of semantic states using physical bitwise operators.
2. **Evaluation:** The measurement of the precise geometric distance (Hamming distance) between user inputs and generated variations.
3. **Selection:** The presentation of topologically optimal states for human confirmation, aiming to ensure the system acts strictly as a transparent extension of user intent.

To support this proposed VES harness, the underlying numerical primitive is theoretically rebuilt in an attempt to resolve four foundational computing bottlenecks.

### 2. Efficiency: Hardware-Aligned Arithmetic

**The Challenge:** Standard computational mathematics relies heavily on IEEE-754 floating-point standards. While versatile, floating-point arithmetic introduces rounding errors and necessitates complex hardware logic to handle boundary conditions (e.g., division by zero, infinite looping during irrational generation).

#### 2.1 The Burden of Floating-Point Exceptions
**[Scientific Consensus]** Representing continuous reality using discretized floating-point scalars forces processors to constantly truncate data. When boundary conditions are met, they trigger exception traps that stall the Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU), consuming excess clock cycles and power.
* **Source 1:** *Gustafson, J. L. (2015). "The End of Error: Unum Computing."* Proves that IEEE 754 floating-point arithmetic is structurally flawed for boundary mathematics, creating compounding errors in dynamic systems that demand heavier computational overhead to correct.
* **Source 2:** *Hennessy, J. L., & Patterson, D. A. (2024). "Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach."* Details the massive ALU stall-cycles generated by hardware exception traps and branch mispredictions during algorithmic loops.

#### 2.2 The Derived Claim: Topological Routing and Integer Scaling
**[Empirical Horizon]** To pursue maximum ALU efficiency on consumer SoCs, the Qn primitive is designed to discard floating-point arrays entirely.
* **Contextual Zoom:** The framework proposes storing mathematical magnitude ($M$) strictly as an absolute integer. Fractional values and precision limits are intended to be governed by the **Contextual Zoom ($z$)**, processed logically as $M \times 10^{-z}$. 
* **Zero-Compute Bypass:** The Qn is structured to integrate an Operational Set ($Op$) capable of topological routing. Constants such as $Q0$ (Origin) and $Q1$ (Unit) are redefined geometrically as mirrors. The goal is for operations that traditionally yield an undefined state (e.g., $X \div 0$) to be intercepted by the $Op$ set before reaching the ALU, returning a defined geometric reflection. Irrational computations are hypothesized to halt logically through "Latent Generators," aiming to preserve precision symbolically until explicitly forced by the context.

### 3. Performance: Mitigating the Serialization Tax

**The Challenge:** Data structures are traditionally contextualized by their storage medium. Relational databases on disk must be parsed, deserialized into graphs or arrays in Random Access Memory (RAM), and then loaded into CPU registers. 

#### 3.1 The Von Neumann Bottleneck and Serialization Overhead
**[Scientific Consensus]** The separation of data structures across the memory hierarchy creates a systemic "serialization tax." Data must be unpacked, translated, and re-packed across the system bus, meaning that data movement now frequently consumes substantially more energy and time than the actual computation.
* **Source 1:** *Mutlu, O. (2025). "Memory-Centric Computing Architectures."* Quantifies the Von Neumann Bottleneck, noting that moving data off-chip dictates the upper performance boundaries of modern hardware.
* **Source 2:** *Barroso, L. A., et al. (2013). "The Datacenter as a Computer."* Demonstrates that protocol buffering and the serialization/deserialization of passive data structures consume a disproportionately massive fraction of global compute cycles.

#### 3.2 The Derived Claim: The Quadripartite Matrix and Cold Storage
**[Empirical Horizon]** It is hypothesized that performance is maximized when a data structure is structurally isomorphic across all storage mediums. The Qn primitive seeks to achieve this through strict dimensional bounding.
* **The 64-Byte Bounding:** A single Qn is theoretically formulated as a **Quadripartite Matrix** measuring exactly 512 bits (64 Bytes), aligning perfectly with the standard L1 Cache line of modern microprocessors. 
* **The Matrix Partitions:** It divides into the Maths Set ($M$), Semantic Set ($S$), Operational Set ($Op$), and Constraint Set ($Halt$).
* **Cold Storage First:** Because exactly 64 Qns are calculated to fit within a standard 4KB NVMe SSD storage sector, the proposed system employs an Append-Only disk paradigm. Non-volatile storage acts as the permanent ledger. RAM is envisioned to serve strictly as a zero-copy projection layer, applying transient contextual matrices (such as local zoom levels) without mutating the underlying disk primitive, with the explicit goal of removing serialization overhead.

### 4. Modularity: Deterministic Semantic Topology

**The Challenge:** Mapping human semantics (natural language, logic, relationships) to binary architecture currently relies on statistical models. These models map relationships based on proximity within training data, which can result in non-deterministic outputs where the machine cannot reliably distinguish between physical absolutes and subjective narrative.

#### 4.1 Hallucinations and Distributed Vector Representations
**[Scientific Consensus]** Deep learning models map relationships probabilistically, leading to structural unreliability (often termed hallucinations). Conversely, Vector Symbolic Architectures (VSA) demonstrate that high-dimensional distributed vectors can encode compositional logic deterministically using simple bitwise operators.
* **Source 1:** *Ji, Z., et al. (2023). "Survey of Hallucination in Natural Language Generation."* Systematically outlines how stochastic text generation architectures inherently fail to ground outputs in objective factual reality.
* **Source 2:** *Kanerva, P. (2009). "Hyperdimensional Computing."* Demonstrates that cognitive operations and contextual binding can be executed deterministically using massive binary vectors rather than floating-point matrices.

#### 4.2 The Derived Claim: VSA and Dual-State Anchoring
**[Empirical Horizon]** To pursue modularity and deterministic reasoning, the Qn primitive is designed to utilize a 448-bit Semantic Hypervector. 
* **Hardware-Level Processing:** The theoretical design intends for combining concepts to utilize single-cycle hardware operations (e.g., Bitwise XOR), and evaluating relatedness to utilize the physical Hamming Distance (POPCNT), yielding an objective geometric measurement of semantic proximity.
* **Dual-State Topologies:** The semantic topology proposes a partition into **Primal Qns** (foundational constants like $Q0$, $Q1$, $\pi$, $c$, hardcoded during initialization to provide an objective geometry base) and **Anchor Qns** (generated during localized uptime via prime number allocation to anchor subjective vocabulary).
* **Markov Dependency Chains:** The framework hypothesizes that if complex concepts carry a hashed record of their derivation from Primal Qns, the machine can ground subjective data in deterministic logic, seeking to eliminate statistical hallucinations.

### 5. Security: Physical Provenance and Ledger Integrity

**The Challenge:** In classical computing, numerical data is fungible and weightless. A digital state carries no intrinsic record of its origin, making systems vulnerable to injection, state manipulation, and Sybil attacks. 

#### 5.1 Hardware Trust and Entropy 
**[Scientific Consensus]** True systemic integrity requires tying digital information to physical hardware entropy. Distributed consensus typically requires centralized validation unless data can be immutably tied to the physical thermodynamic reality of its host hardware.
* **Source 1:** *Maes, R. (2013/2024). "Physically Unclonable Functions: Foundations and Applications."* Establishes that true digital identity must be anchored to thermodynamic stochasticity and uncontrollable physical manufacturing variations.
* **Source 2:** *NIST IR 8401 (2022/2024 Update).* Identifies that securing information boundaries requires hardware-enabled trust mechanisms natively integrated into the root computing layer.

#### 5.2 The Derived Claim: Monotonic Birth Sequences and PUFs
**[Empirical Horizon]** The CDQN system is designed to regulate data creation through a strict chronological protocol, intending to move security from external software barriers into the numerical primitive itself.
* **Monotonic Birth:** The hardware node is envisioned to generate Qn primitives at a controlled, sequential rate (e.g., 1 Qn per second of uptime).
* **Proof of Birth (PoB):** Upon generation, the Qn's Constraint Set is structured to be imprinted with a Physical Unclonable Function (PUF) signature, derived from localized, non-replicable thermodynamic noise (SoC thermal drift and clock jitter).

This physical provenance, combined with the proposed Append-Only NVMe ledger, aims to guarantee that data cannot be retrospectively altered or spoofed. The hypothesis posits that nodes can achieve trustless consensus not by comparing raw values, but by validating the Markov Dependency Chains against the universally shared Primal Qns. Furthermore, this architecture aims to natively support advanced data privacy frameworks by eventually allowing for Vector Cryptographic Erasure (crypto-shredding) of sensitive semantic data while theoretically preserving the structural integrity of the chronological chain.

### 6. Conclusion and Immediate Roadmap

The transition toward affordable, sovereign semantic computation necessitates an exploration into rethinking the atomic unit of data processing. The Quang Number (Qn) primitive provides a theoretical framework designed specifically for the physical constraints and capabilities of contemporary commodity hardware. 

By proposing the unification of magnitude, semantic vectors, operational logic, and physical constraints into a 64-byte Quadripartite Matrix, the Qn architecture is hypothesized to mitigate the Von Neumann bottleneck and offer a deterministic alternative to probabilistic approximations. It aims to establish the necessary foundation for a computational harness (Variation, Evaluation, Selection), allowing edge devices to potentially process human intent securely and efficiently.

Empirical validation and definitive proof of these mechanics remain the purview of the broader scientific and engineering community, pending rigorous testing via the future CDQN system.
