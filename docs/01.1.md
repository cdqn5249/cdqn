### Glossary of Terms

* **CDQN System:** **[Empirical Horizon]** (Chained and Distributed Quang Numbers). A deterministic computational framework envisioned to function as a **Harness of Variation, Evaluation, and Selection (VES)**. It is designed to enable semantic computation on standard hardware without relying on stochastic or autonomous AI models.
* **Contextual Zoom ($z$):** **[Empirical Horizon]** An integer-based scaling mechanism intended to replace floating-point arithmetic. It applies a decimal shift operator ($M \times 10^{-z}$) to a fixed-point magnitude, aiming to preserve absolute precision without hardware overflow.
* **Proof of Birth (PoB):** **[Scientific Consensus / Hardware]** A cryptographic metadata tag utilizing Physical Unclonable Functions (PUFs)—such as localized thermal drift—to anchor a digital state to its chronological creation point in physical silicon.
* **Quadripartite Matrix:** **[Empirical Horizon]** The proposed foundational 64-Byte structure of a Qn, unifying four categorical domains: Maths ($M$), Semantics ($S$), Operations ($Op$), and Constraints ($Halt$).
* **Quang:** A Vietnamese concept translating to *clarity, brightness, and integrity*. It serves as the foundational design philosophy aiming to ensure systemic transparency, affordability, and determinism.
* **Semantic Hypervector:** **[Scientific Consensus]** A high-dimensional binary array (derived from Vector Symbolic Architectures) capable of storing conceptual relationships and Markov Chain dependencies via distributed superposition, evaluated via bitwise operations.
* **Zero-Compute Bypass:** **[Empirical Horizon]** Proposed hardware-level topological routing instructions. Designed to intercept mathematically undefined or static operations (e.g., division by zero) and return geometric reflections, with the goal of bypassing Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) iterative execution.

### Abstract

Modern computational architectures face compounding challenges regarding energy efficiency, memory bandwidth, and deterministic semantic processing. The current reliance on probabilistic Large Language Models (LLMs) requires substantial, centralized hardware clusters, creating a barrier to localized, sovereign computation. Furthermore, legacy data structures are known to exacerbate the Von Neumann bottleneck through intensive memory serialization and floating-point exception handling. This paper introduces the theoretical foundation of the **Quang Number (Qn)** primitive: a hardware-aligned, 64-byte computational unit designed to natively process semantic topology. By unifying magnitude, semantic vectors, operational routing, and cryptographic bounds into a single, strictly partitioned, L1 Cache-aligned matrix, the Qn framework seeks to maximize affordability and deployment on consumer-grade Systems-on-a-Chip (SoCs). The Qn primitive is proposed as the foundation for the CDQN System, a deterministic harness structured around Variation, Evaluation, and Selection. This theoretical architecture pursues four primary technical objectives: Efficiency (hardware alignment), Performance (serialization mitigation), Modularity (deterministic semantics), and Security (physical provenance).

### 1. Introduction: Affordability and the Deterministic Harness

The evolution of contemporary computer science has heavily prioritized abstraction and emergent capability, culminating in the widespread adoption of stochastic neural networks. While these models effectively approximate natural language processing, their reliance on billion-parameter matrices necessitates centralized, high-energy hardware infrastructure, limiting the deployment of advanced semantic computation on edge devices.

#### 1.1 The Centralization of Semantic Computation
**[Scientific Consensus]** The computational limits of deep learning present severe economic and environmental constraints. The dependency on centralized GPU clusters creates a computational monopoly, while the probabilistic nature of LLMs prevents deterministic guarantees of accuracy. 
* **Source 1:** *Thompson et al. (2020), "The Computational Limits of Deep Learning."* Demonstrates that the computational power required to sustain deep learning performance improvements is scaling at an unsustainable, exponentially cost-prohibitive rate.
* **Source 2:** *Bender et al. (2021), "On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots."* Outlines the inherent limitations of massive language models, specifically their inability to ground text in objective semantic meaning without generating probabilistic output.

#### 1.2 The Derived Claim: The CDQN VES Harness
**[Empirical Horizon]** To ensure equitable access to high-level semantic tools, computation must ideally be executable on affordable, commodity silicon (e.g., consumer-grade processors and localized storage). Achieving this requires exploring a shift from probabilistic inference to deterministic calculation.

The proposed theoretical solution is the **Quang Number (Qn)** primitive, intended to be utilized within the **CDQN System**. The CDQN is envisioned not as an autonomous artificial intelligence, but as a computational harness governed by three deterministic principles:
1. **Variation:** The ability to generate mathematical combinations of semantic states using physical bitwise operators.
2. **Evaluation:** The measurement of the precise geometric distance (Hamming distance) between user inputs and generated variations.
3. **Selection:** The presentation of topologically optimal states for human confirmation, aiming to ensure the system acts strictly as a transparent extension of user intent.

To support this proposed VES harness, the underlying numerical primitive is theoretically rebuilt in an attempt to resolve four foundational computing bottlenecks.

### 2. Efficiency: Hardware-Aligned Arithmetic

**The Challenge:** Standard computational mathematics relies heavily on IEEE-754 floating-point standards. While versatile, floating-point arithmetic introduces rounding errors and necessitates complex hardware logic to handle boundary conditions. 

#### 2.1 The Burden of Floating-Point Exceptions and Extensibility
**[Scientific Consensus]** Representing continuous reality using discretized floating-point scalars forces processors to constantly truncate data. When boundary conditions are met, they trigger exception traps that stall the Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU). Furthermore, implementing novel routing paradigms natively requires programmable instruction sets or kernel-level hooks.
* **Source 1:** *Gustafson, J. L. (2015). "The End of Error: Unum Computing."* Proves that IEEE 754 floating-point arithmetic is structurally flawed for boundary mathematics, creating compounding errors in dynamic systems.
* **Source 2:** *Hennessy, J. L., & Patterson, D. A. (2024).* Details the massive ALU stall-cycles generated by hardware exception traps and branch mispredictions.
* **Source 3:** *Higham, N. J. (2002).* Establishes that numerical stability and accuracy demand rigorous fixed-point interval arithmetic bounds to prevent overflow during cascaded operations.
* **Source 4:** *McKeown, N. et al. (2023) / Waterman & Asanović (2020).* Demonstrates that modern custom Instruction Set Architectures (ISA), such as RISC-V, and programmable data planes (eBPF) provide feasible pathways for hardware-level topological routing.

#### 2.2 The Derived Claim: Topological Routing and Interval Scaling
**[Empirical Horizon]** To pursue maximum ALU efficiency on consumer SoCs, the Qn primitive is designed to discard floating-point arrays entirely.
* **Contextual Zoom & Interval Bounds:** The framework proposes storing mathematical magnitude ($M$) strictly as an absolute integer. Fractional values are governed by the **Contextual Zoom ($z$)**, processed logically as $M \times 10^{-z}$. To theoretically guarantee precision during chained operations, the system posits the adoption of fixed-point interval arithmetic, bounding overflow and underflow conditions safely within the $M$ set.
* **Zero-Compute Bypass:** The Qn integrates an Operational Set ($Op$) capable of topological routing. Constants such as $Q0$ (Origin) and $Q1$ (Unit) are redefined geometrically as mirrors. The goal is for undefined operations (e.g., $X \div 0$) to be intercepted by the $Op$ set before reaching the ALU. The physical implementation of this bypass is posited to eventually map onto custom ISA extensions (e.g., RISC-V opcodes) and eBPF-style kernel hooks, the engineering of which is formally deferred to Series 03.

### 3. Performance: Mitigating the Serialization Tax

**The Challenge:** Data structures are traditionally contextualized by their storage medium, creating a systemic "serialization tax" when moving data from disk to RAM to CPU.

#### 3.1 The Von Neumann Bottleneck and Serialization Overhead
**[Scientific Consensus]** The separation of data structures across the memory hierarchy creates immense latency. Data movement frequently consumes substantially more energy and time than actual computation.
* **Source 1:** *Mutlu, O. (2025). "Memory-Centric Computing Architectures."* Quantifies the Von Neumann Bottleneck, noting off-chip data movement dictates modern hardware performance limits.
* **Source 2:** *Barroso, L. A., et al. (2013). "The Datacenter as a Computer."* Demonstrates that protocol buffering and serialization of passive data structures consume a disproportionately massive fraction of global compute cycles.

#### 3.2 The Derived Claim: The Exact Quadripartite Bit-Matrix
**[Empirical Horizon]** It is hypothesized that performance is maximized when a data structure is structurally isomorphic across all storage mediums. The Qn primitive seeks to achieve this through strict dimensional bounding.

The single Qn is theoretically formulated as a **Quadripartite Matrix** measuring exactly 512 bits (64 Bytes), aligning perfectly with the standard L1 Cache line. To ensure mathematical and hardware feasibility, the precise bit-partition is posited as follows:
1. **Maths Set ($M$):** 64 bits (Absolute magnitude integer).
2. **Semantic Set ($S$):** 384 bits (High-dimensional representation and Markov hash).
3. **Operational Set ($Op$):** 32 bits (Topology routing and modifiers).
4. **Constraint Set ($Halt$):** 32 bits (Security limits, PoB metadata).

Because exactly 64 Qns calculate to fit within a standard 4KB NVMe SSD storage sector, the proposed system employs an Append-Only disk paradigm. RAM is envisioned to serve strictly as a zero-copy projection layer, aiming to remove serialization overhead entirely. *(Note: Formal cross-boundary performance modeling using hardware performance counters is deferred to Series 04 benchmarks).*

### 4. Modularity: Deterministic Semantic Topology

**The Challenge:** Mapping human semantics to binary architecture currently relies on statistical models, which can result in non-deterministic "hallucinations" where the machine cannot reliably distinguish between physical absolutes and subjective narrative.

#### 4.1 Hallucinations and Distributed Vector Representations
**[Scientific Consensus]** Deep learning models map relationships probabilistically, leading to structural unreliability. Conversely, Vector Symbolic Architectures (VSA) demonstrate that high-dimensional distributed vectors can encode compositional logic deterministically using simple bitwise operators.
* **Source 1:** *Ji, Z., et al. (2023). "Survey of Hallucination in Natural Language Generation."* Systematically outlines how stochastic text generation architectures inherently fail to ground outputs in objective factual reality.
* **Source 2:** *Kanerva, P. (2009). "Hyperdimensional Computing."* Demonstrates that cognitive operations and contextual binding can be executed deterministically using massive binary vectors.

#### 4.2 The Derived Claim: VSA Binding and Dual-State Anchoring
**[Empirical Horizon]** To pursue modularity and deterministic reasoning, the Qn primitive utilizes the 384-bit Semantic Hypervector ($S$). 
* **Algebraic Binding:** To mathematically guarantee reversible semantic composition and unbinding without vector collision, the theoretical design intends for combining concepts to utilize single-cycle hardware operations such as **Bitwise XOR coupled with circular bit-shifting** (building upon Kanerva's VSA framework). Evaluating relatedness utilizes the physical Hamming Distance (POPCNT).
* **Dual-State Topologies & Markov Chains:** The semantic topology proposes a partition into **Primal Qns** (hardcoded objective constants like $Q0$, $\pi$) and **Anchor Qns** (subjective prime-number anchors). By ensuring complex concepts carry a hashed Markov Dependency Chain of their derivation from Primal Qns, the machine aims to ground subjective data in deterministic logic.

### 5. Security: Physical Provenance and Ledger Integrity

**The Challenge:** In classical computing, numerical data is fungible. A digital state carries no intrinsic record of its origin, making systems vulnerable to injection and Sybil attacks. 

#### 5.1 Hardware Trust and Entropy Pooling
**[Scientific Consensus]** True systemic integrity requires tying digital information to physical hardware entropy. Extracting this entropy at high frequencies without introducing latency requires robust buffering.
* **Source 1:** *Maes, R. (2013/2024).* Establishes that true digital identity must be anchored to thermodynamic stochasticity.
* **Source 2:** *NIST IR 8401 (2022/2024 Update).* Identifies that securing information boundaries requires hardware-enabled trust mechanisms.
* **Source 3:** *Delvaux, J. et al. (2015).* Demonstrates architectures for lightweight, efficient PUF deployment and entropy buffering.
* **Source 4:** *Schindler, W. & Standaert, F. (2010).* Establishes constant-time extraction protocols to ensure side-channel resistance.

#### 5.2 The Derived Claim: Monotonic Birth Sequences and Entropy Buffering
**[Empirical Horizon]** The CDQN system is designed to regulate data creation through a strict chronological protocol.
* **Monotonic Birth:** The hardware node is envisioned to generate Qn primitives sequentially (e.g., 1 Qn per second of uptime). 
* **Proof of Birth (PoB) & Buffering:** Upon generation, the Qn's Constraint Set is structured to be imprinted with a localized PUF signature. To prevent latency bottlenecks during PUF extraction, the architecture theoretically incorporates a continuous **entropy buffering and pooling mechanism**. This constant-time enrollment aims to ensure physical security does not compromise the system's generation throughput.

This physical provenance, combined with the proposed Append-Only NVMe ledger, aims to guarantee that data cannot be retrospectively altered.

### 6. Conclusion and Immediate Roadmap

The transition toward affordable, sovereign semantic computation necessitates an exploration into rethinking the atomic unit of data processing. The Quang Number (Qn) primitive provides a theoretical framework designed specifically for the physical constraints of contemporary commodity hardware. 

By proposing the unification of magnitude, semantic vectors, operational logic, and physical constraints into a precisely partitioned 64-byte Quadripartite Matrix, the Qn architecture is hypothesized to mitigate the Von Neumann bottleneck. It aims to establish the necessary foundation for a computational harness (Variation, Evaluation, Selection), allowing edge devices to potentially process human intent deterministically.

It is critical to note that the capabilities outlined in this text represent structural intents and design goals. Empirical validation and definitive proof of these mechanics remain the purview of the broader scientific and engineering community, pending rigorous testing via the future CDQN system.
