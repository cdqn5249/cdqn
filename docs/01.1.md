The bit was designed for an era of low-density information transmission where the primary challenge was the reliable conveyance of discrete signals. However, as we have scaled this abstraction to the level of global neural networks and multi-terabyte datasets, the abstraction has become a liability. The current computational model treats data as a "black box" of magnitudes, forcing the machine to expend massive energy and temporal resources to re-induce context at every layer of the software stack. 

The costs of this "meaning-blind" foundation have become an existential threat to computational progress. The energy required to move meaningless scalars across a motherboard, the compute cycles wasted on re-establishing context, and the societal risks of "hallucinations" in autonomous systems demand a new approach. The Quang Number (Qn) **[Empirical Horizon]** is a mathematical intervention designed to restore **Provenance** and **Lineage** to the heart of the computational unit. ### 2. The Physical Impasse: Thermodynamics and Architectural Entropy

The first necessity for the Qn lies in the harsh reality of semiconductor physics. For decades, the industry followed the predictable path of Moore’s Law and Dennard Scaling. This path has ended.

#### 2.1 The Power Wall and the Failure of CMOS Scaling
Historically, as transistors shrank, their power density remained constant because the operating voltage could be lowered proportionally. However, as detailed in **Hennessy & Patterson (2024)** and supported by the seminal work of **Esmaeilzadeh et al. (2011)** on the end of multicore scaling, as we moved into sub-3nm nodes, this scaling law collapsed. At these atomic scales, threshold voltage cannot be lowered further without electron leakage currents becoming unmanageable. We have hit the "Power Wall."

Modern chips are now governed by the phenomenon of "Dark Silicon," where significant portions of the circuitry must remain powered off to prevent thermal destruction. Legacy binary computation, which requires billions of discrete bit-flips for even basic relational tasks, is the primary driver of this heat. The Qn **[Empirical Horizon]** resolves this by being "Information-Dense"—a property defined as the ability to pack multiple semantic and logical constraints into a single machine word. By reducing the number of instructions required to resolve complex logic through a tri-dimensional primitive, the Qn effectively cools the processor through mathematical efficiency rather than physical refrigeration.

#### 2.2 The Von Neumann Bottleneck and the Entropy of Data Movement
The second physical impasse is the architectural separation of logic and memory—the "Von Neumann Bottleneck." As noted by **Backus (1977)** and reiterated in modern systems audits, moving a single piece of data between the RAM and the CPU costs between 100 to 1,000 times more energy than the actual computation performed on that data. Empirical data provided by **Horowitz (2014)** in his ISSCC keynote confirms that a 64-bit double-precision FMA (Fused Multiply-Add) operation consumes ~20 pJ, while a DRAM read consumes ~16,000 pJ—an 800× energy penalty.

The Qn **[Empirical Horizon]** resolves this through a strategy influenced by the research of **Ainsworth & Jones (2017)** regarding **Graph-Aware Prefetching**. Because a Qn carries its own narrative and logic dependencies within its 64-bit word, the hardware no longer fetches "blind bytes." It fetches "Relational Clusters." By making the number "smarter," we drastically reduce the volume of data that must traverse the bus, providing a direct temporal and energy gain.
