## 1. Introduction: From Processing to Manifestation

### 1.1 The Epistemological Failure of the Black Box
The legacy of the Turing machine is the illusion of the "Black Box." In contemporary Computer Science (CS), the dominant paradigm relies on Symbolic Processing: an architecture where discrete, weightless symbols are processed by a disconnected arithmetic logic unit. As we transition to an era where autonomous agents are tasked with managing critical physical infrastructure and executing long-horizon engineering workflows, this epistemological fragility becomes a terminal liability. 

To solve the crises outlined in Paper 01a, the fundamental objective of computation must shift from *Symbolic Processing* (predicting the next sequence on a linear tape) to **Stoichiometric Manifestation** (verifiably altering a physical and logical state within a conserved geometric manifold). A sovereign machine must be capable of verifying its own state, defending its logic from injection, and accounting for the thermodynamic cost of its computations.

### 1.2 The Hardware-Agnostic Transition Phase
The transition to the SRM era cannot await the global fabrication of native, geometrically-aligned QS silicon processors. The architecture requires a transition phase. Consequently, the SRM is specified as a hardware-agnostic framework. It must be capable of operating as a "Virtual Lattice Layer" deployed atop standard consumer silicon (x86, ARM architectures). To preserve stoichiometric integrity and prevent software-level spoofing by compromised legacy operating systems, the SRM must execute at the bare-metal hypervisor level (Ring -1) or within Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs). This deployment strategy guarantees the unforgeability of its physical anchors while utilizing legacy hardware, providing the functional bridge required before native QS hardware is realized in Series 04.
