#### **Non-Commercial Creative & Learning Uses**

#### **Passive Consumption**

Simple viewing, listening, or sharing of the Work without adding new insight, meaning, or expression is not permitted under this license. This means, for instance, you cannot simply re-post the Work without any modification or commentary for public access.

#### **Production Use (Innovation Tier - Free Commercial Use)**

Commercial use is permitted without payment if:
*   Your organization earns less than $10M/year, OR
*   Your initiative/project is under 5 years old.

"Commercial use" includes any case where the Work generates income, visibility, or branding—directly or indirectly.

Non-profit use is allowed if it does not involve monetization or donor conversion. For non-profit organizations, "donor conversion" refers to the direct use of the Work in materials or campaigns specifically designed to solicit and convert potential donors into financial contributors. While general mission-related use is permitted, using the Work as a direct incentive for donations or as a primary component of a fundraising campaign would typically fall under "donor conversion" and thus require a commercial partnership.

### **3. Commercial Partnership for Scale**

You must form a Commercial Partnership Agreement if:
*   You exceed 1,000 distributed units (non-automated/manual scale), OR
*   You exceed 100,000 machine-integrated reproductions (machine-generated, embedded, or network deployed).

#### **Definitions**

*   **Distributed units:** Copies given, published, or sold (print, file, binary). This refers to individual instances of the Work being physically or digitally transferred to end-users (e.g., each installation of software, each physical book sold).
*   **Machine-integrated reproductions:** Any automated, embedded, or AI-powered redistributions. This applies when the Work is integrated into a system that then generates or distributes copies at scale. This includes, but is not limited to:
    *   Using the Work in AI model training where the model generates and distributes new derived content at scale.
    *   Automated content generation platforms that incorporate the Work and produce numerous outputs.
    *   Integration into embedded systems or devices where the Work is replicated across multiple units (e.g., a sound file on smart home devices, a design pattern in 3D printable objects).
    *   Network deployments (e.g., websites) where the Work is hosted and accessed by a large number of unique users, with each instance of the work being loaded or viewed typically counting as a reproduction if it is part of an automated distribution system.

#### **Examples of Trigger Points:**
*   A book sells 1,500 copies – partnership required.
*   A design pattern generates 200,000 3D print runs via AI – partnership required.
*   An audio sample is used in 300,000 smart home devices – partnership required.
*   An image embedded on a website is viewed by 150,000 unique visitors, where the website automatically serves the image to each visitor – partnership likely required due to machine-integrated reproductions.
*   An AI model, trained on licensed content, generates and distributes 120,000 unique stories - partnership required.

#### **Partnership Options**

Partnership agreements may include:
*   Revenue sharing
*   Subscription or site license
*   Escrow or continuity guarantees
*   Co-branding or roadmap collaboration
*   IP indemnification
*   Commercial trust terms

#### **Explanation of Partnership Terms:**
*   **IP indemnification:** An agreement where one party (the licensee) protects the other (the licensor) from legal liability, costs, or damages arising from the licensee's use of the intellectual property.
*   **Commercial trust terms:** Specific conditions designed to build and maintain trust, often detailing transparency, audit rights, and mutual responsibilities in handling intellectual property.
